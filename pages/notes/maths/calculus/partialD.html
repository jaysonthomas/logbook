Partial derivative

Symbol

$\frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}} \bigg|_{g(y)} $

Components

  • $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}$: For a tiny move in $x$-direction, how much does the value of $f$ change? (assuming $y$ stays constant during that tiny step).

  • $\bigg|$: This means evaluated at or restricted to.

  • $g(y)$: This is the constraint. Evaluation of the derivative is restricted to a specific curve or set of values defined by $g(y)$ in the $xy$-plane.

Visual Intuition

$f(x, y)$ is a mountain range.

$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}$ is the steepness of the mountain if say one walks strictly East-West.The constraint $g(y)$ is a hiking trail winding through the mountains.

Combining the two, what is the East-West steepness at every step, specifically along the trail $g(y)$.

It is the slope experienced under specific conditions (e.g., "What is the pressure change relative to volume, given that the temperature is held constant?").

Example

If $g(y)$ defines a specific $x$-value (i.e., $x = g(y)$), then this relationship is essentially being substituted into the derivative.

If $f(x, y) = x^2 + y^2$ and the constraint is $x = y^3$:

  1. First, find the general derivative: $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2x$.
  2. Then, evaluate it at the constraint: $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \big|_{x=y^3} = 2(y^3) = 2y^3$.
In this case, the "steepness" in the $x$-direction is now expressed entirely in terms of the position along the $y$-axis.
