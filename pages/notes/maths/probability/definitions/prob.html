Definitions: Probability

Probability

$$ P(\text{of a particular event}) = \frac{\text{Frequency of the particular event occurring}}{\text{Total occurrences of all events}} $$

Continuous samples space

The real value position of a robot, represented by a random variable, is an example of a continuous sample space.
For a continuous sample space, the probability of any particular value will always be 0. For example, probability that a vehicle is in some region is given more importance than any specific location.

Example

Consider running a test to see if a bot comes to a stop at exactly the right point, a 1000 times. At the end of each test, the bot's position is measured w.r.t. the target.

$$ P(Bot\, stops\, 1mm\, from\, target) = \frac{Frequency\, of\, particular\, event} {Total\, number\, of\, events} = \frac{900}{1000} = 0.9 $$ Random variable, $X$, represents the event, i.e. the error in the final position.
Example, $x_2 = 2000$, $P(X=x_2) = 0.05$.

Definitions related to probability

Term Definition
Event or outcome Something that could happen
Sample space List of events
Discrete sample space Discrete set of events or possible outcomes
Probability Relative likelihood or relative frequency of an event occurring
Normalised PD A PD in which all the probabilities sum to 1
Probability of 0 The event is guaranteed to not happen
Probability of 1 The event is certain to happen
Conditional probability The probability of some event given that some other event already occurred. PMF is used to find this.
$p(x)$ The probability of a vehicle being in position $x$.
$p(x,y)$ The probability of a vehicle being in position x and making measurements y.
$p(y|x)$ The probability of making measurements y given that a vehicle is in position x.
