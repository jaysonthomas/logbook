Norm

Euclidean

Also called $L^2$ norm. It gives the straight line ('as the crow flies') distance between two points in space.

For a vector $v = [v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n]$, the formula is: $$ \|v\|_2 = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} |v_i|^2} $$

For a complex number $z = a + bi$:
  • Simple square: $z^2 = (a + bi)^2 = a^2 - b^2 + 2abi$ (This can be negative or complex).
  • Magnitude squared: $|z|^2 = z \cdot \bar{z} = a^2 + b^2$ (This is always a positive real number).
By using $|v_i|^2$, the formula ensures that the result is always a positive real scalar, which is a fundamental requirement for any "norm" to be valid.
