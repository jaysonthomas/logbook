Jacobian Example: Robotic arm

Planar 1R Arm

  • Input space variable: Angle $\theta$
  • Output space: One 2-dimensional point in $x, y$
The Jacobian would be given by: $$\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial{f_x(\theta)}}{\partial{\theta}} \\ \frac{\partial{f_y(\theta)}}{\partial{\theta}} \end{bmatrix}$$ $f_x(\theta), f_y(\theta)$ alone gives the $x, y$ point. Dividing/normalising by the change in the input space variable gives the change in the output space due to the change in the input space.

2R arm

The input space consists of 2 variables $\theta_1, \theta_2$ and the output space consists of the end point of the end effector. The Jacobian would be: $$\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial{f_x}}{\partial{\theta_1}} & \frac{\partial{f_x}}{\partial{\theta_2}} \\ \frac{\partial{f_y}}{\partial{\theta_1}} & \frac{\partial{f_y}}{\partial{\theta_2}} \end{bmatrix}$$ $f_x(\theta), f_y(\theta)$, algebraically, gives the position of the end point in $x,y$ respectively, given the 2 joint angles.
