Lagrange multiplier

Overview

If two vectors are parallel, one is simply a multiple of the other. $$ \nabla{f} = k \nabla{c} $$ To turn this into an equilibrium equation (the kind mathematicians and physicists love because they represent a balance), everything is moved to one side: $$\begin{align*} \nabla{f} - k \nabla{c} &= 0 \\ \nabla{f} + \lambda \nabla{c} &= 0 \end{align*}$$ This is the classic Lagrange expression. At a specific point, the force pulling the function $f$ to change its value is perfectly balanced by the reaction force of the constraint $c$.

$\lambda$ acts as a scaling factor that stretches or shrinks $\nabla c$ to perfectly cancel out $\nabla f$.
