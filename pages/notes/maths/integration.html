Double integral

Taking the fourier transform of the convolution integral: $$ \mathcal{F}((f*g)(t)) = \int^\infty_{-\infty} \left[ \int^\infty_{-\infty}f(\tau)g(t-\tau)d\tau \right] e^{-j\omega t} dt $$

Rearranging the limits

Here, the summation of the inner integral is done w.r.t. $\tau$ and that of the outer integral is done w.r.t. $t$. A double integral can be integrated in either order as long as the integration limits are transformed appropriately.

Here, the limits are both from $-\infty$ to $\infty$, so rearranging the integrals is just a matter of pulling $e^{-j\omega t}dt$ in and $d\tau$ out.

$$ \mathcal{F}((f*g)(t)) = \int^\infty_{-\infty} \left[ \int^\infty_{-\infty}f(\tau)g(t-\tau) e^{-j\omega t} dt \right] d\tau $$ $f(\tau)$ of the inner integral can be moved out since it is a function of $\tau$ and hence a constant when integrated w.r.t. $t$. $$ \mathcal{F}((f*g)(t)) = \int^\infty_{-\infty} \left[ f(\tau)\int^\infty_{-\infty}g(t-\tau) e^{-j\omega t} dt \right] d\tau $$
