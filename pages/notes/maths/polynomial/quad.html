Quadratic equation

Description

The quadratic Lagrange polynomial can always be expanded and rewritten in the standard form: $$ L_2(x) = ax^2 + bx + c $$ where the coefficients depend on the values $f(x_0), f(x_1), f(x_2)$ and the locations of the interpolation points x_0, x_1, x_2.

The Lagrange form is specific to interpolation and is built to satisfy the condition: $$ p(x_i) = f(x_i) \quad\quad i=0,1,2 $$ This means it guarantees that the polynomial passes through the three specified points.

The standard quadratic form $ax^2 + bx + c$ on the other hand, does not explicitly emphasize the interpolation property but represents the same class of curves, a parabola.

Intuition about the coefficie —nts

$a$

  • Determines the shape of the parabola
    • If $a>0$, the parabola opens upwards. If $a<0$, it opens downwards.

  • Non-zero requirement
    • If $a=0$, the equation becomes linear ($bx+c=0$), which is not quadratic.

  • Affects the width of the parabola
    • A larger $|a|$ makes the parabola narrower. A smaller $|a|$ (close to but not equal to 0) makes the parabola wider.

Others

  • $b$ affects the slope of the curve at the vertex
  • $c$ affects the y-intercept
