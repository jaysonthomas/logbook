Direction Cosine Matrix

Overview

We'll be rotating the world frame to the object frame, i.e. $^OR^W$. The default in the manipulation library is $^WR^O$ given by RotationMatrix.MakeYRotation(np.pi/2) for example, to rotate the object frame by $90^0$ about the y-axis, in order to align with the world frame.
$^WR^O$ $^OR^W$
$Ry = \begin{bmatrix} c & 0 & s \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -s & 0 & c \end{bmatrix}$ $Ry = \begin{bmatrix} c & 0 & -s \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ s & 0 & c \end{bmatrix}$
$Rx = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & c & -s \\ 0 & s & c \end{bmatrix}$ $Rx = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & c & s \\ 0 & -s & c \end{bmatrix}$
$Rz = \begin{bmatrix} c & -s & 0 \\ s & c & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ $Rz = \begin{bmatrix} c & s & 0 \\ -s & c & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$
