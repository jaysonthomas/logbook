Matrix/vector operations related to robotic arms

Topics

In linear algebra, two vectors in an inner product space are orthonormal if they are orthogonal unit vectors. A unit vector means that the vector has a length of 1, which is also known as normalised. Orthogonal means that the two vectors are perpendicular to each other. A set of vectors form an orthonormal set if all vectors in the set are mutually orthogonal and all of unit length. An orthonormal set which forms a basis is called an orthonormal basis.

Example

Consider a rotation matrix representing a 90° rotation around the $z$-axis: $$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ Orthonormality checks:
  • Dot Product of Columns (Orthogonality)
    • Dot product of any two distinct columns is 0. For example, Column 1 $\cdot$ Column 2: $$(0 \times -1) + (1 \times 0) + (0 \times 0) = 0$$

  • Magnitude of Columns (Normalisation)
    • The length (Euclidean norm) of every column is 1. For Column 1: $$\sqrt{0^2 + 1^2 + 0^2} = 1$$
