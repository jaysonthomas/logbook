Exponential functions

Real exponents

For exponential functions that have real numbers for exponents:
  • Negative indicates exponentially decaying signals
  • Positive indicates exponentially growing signals
$$ f(\sigma) = e^{\sigma t} $$ $\sigma$ represents a real number.
As with $\omega$ giving the location of a signal on a frequency line, $\sigma$ gives the location of an exponential function on a real number line. As an exponential signal moves away from the origin, the absolute value of the real number becomes larger and thus the signal decays or grows at a faster rate.

Imaginary exponents

[Jlib]

Exponential functions that have imaginary exponents, such as $e^{j2t}$, produce complex sinusoids through Euler's formula: $$ e^{j\omega t} = cos(\omega t)+ jsin(\omega t) $$ The resulting sinusoid is 2D but not in a spatial sense. Its real (plotted on x-axis) and imaginary (y-axis) components oscillate sinusoidally and are related to each other. The resulting trajectory forms a circle when plotted in the complex plane, as the magnitude of $e^{j2t}$ is constant (equal to 1).

Imaginary and real exponent

$$\begin{align*} e^{st} &= e^{(\sigma + j\omega)t} \\ &= e^{\sigma t} e^{j\omega t} \end{align*}$$
The function $e^{st}$ produces a signal that has both an exponential and a sinusoidal component.
