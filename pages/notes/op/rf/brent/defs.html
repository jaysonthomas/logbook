Brent's method: definitions

Points

$a,b$ are abscissa points that satisfy the following conditions:
  • $a$ and $b$ bracket the sign change of $f(x)$ and hence provides the midpoint

  • $|f(b)| \leq |f(a)|$
    • $b$ always corresponds to the best solution, i.e. the lowest zero, $|f(b)|$, the smallest value of $|f(x)|$ so far.

$c$ is the previous $b$. Hence, $c$ and $b$ provide the secant. $$ c = b_{i-1} $$ $d$ gives an indication of how much the candidate abscissa changes through every iteration. $$\begin{align*} d &= b - c \\ &= \Delta{b} \end{align*}$$
