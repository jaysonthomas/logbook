Brent's method: Reassignments

Overview

Points are reassigned at the start of every iteration based on 2 checks. If either of the 2 checks are satisfied, the next approach would be bisection or secant.

Check 1: Sign

$$ \frac{|f(b)|}{f(b)} == \frac{|f(a)|}{f(a)} $$ If $f(b)$ has changed sign, $a$ is dropped and set to the previous value of $b$, i.e. $c$. $$\begin{align*} a &= c \qquad\qquad f(a) = f(c) \\ d &= b - c \\ e &= d \end{align*}$$ $e$ needs to be assigned before $d$ changes?. $d$ is referred as step and $e$ as prior step.

Check 2: Magnitude

This is the bisection method check of keeping $|f(b)|$ the lowest absolute ordinate value. $$ |f(a)| < |f(b)| $$ If the above condition is met, swap the 2 points and drop $c$. $$\begin{array}{} \begin{aligned} c &= b \\ b &= a \\ a &= c \\ \end{aligned} && \begin{aligned} f(c) &= f(b) \\ f(b) &= f(a) \\ f(a) &= f(c) \\ \end{aligned} \end{array}$$ No changes are made to $e$ and $d$.
