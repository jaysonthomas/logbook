Newton's method

Overview

$$ \Delta{x} = -\left(\frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}}\bigg|_x\right)^{-1} \, f(x) $$ Here, $f()$ is the residual. $\frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}}$ is the Jacobian of the residual.

A first order taylor expansion of the residual is taken and set to 0, i.e. the equation is solved for the a next residual of ~0. It is then evaluated for different $x_{k+1}$ candidate values.
