Root finding: Description

Overview

[Ref]

Given $f(x)$, find a value of $x$ (abscissa), $x^*$ such that $f(x^*) = 0$. It is sufficient for the scalar output of the function, referred to as the ordinate, to be approximately 0.

There are a few examples where there exists exact methods for finding solutions. For example, the quadratic formula:

$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$ gives an exact method for finding roots of the equation $$ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 $$
