Convenient derivative notations

[Jacobian notation]

$$ \nabla{f(x)} = \left(\frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}}\right)^\top \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times 1} \quad \text{Column vector} $$ Hessians have to be symmetric matrices. $$\begin{align*} \nabla^2{f(x)} &= \frac{\partial}{\partial{x}} \left(\nabla{f(x)}\right) \\ &= \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial{x}^2} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} \end{align*}$$

Taylor expansion

Taking the 2nd order Taylor expansion of $f$ (as long as it's a scalar valued function of a vector) to see where the Hessian matrix comes in: $$ f(x + \Delta{x}) \approx f(x) + \frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}} \, \Delta{x} + \frac{1}{2} \Delta{x}^\top \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial{x}^2} \Delta{x} $$ All this is dimensionally correct.
