Quadratic form

Overview

A quadratic form is a scalar-valued function that looks like: $$ f(x) = x^\top A x $$ If $x$ is a vector in 2D space, the quadratic form describes a surface or a bowl shape.

Definiteness

  • Negative Definite
    • If $f(x) < 0$, for every non-zero vector $x$, the shape is like an upside-down bowl (a maximum point at the origin).

  • Positive definite
    • If $f(x) > 0$, it's a right-side-up bowl (a minimum point at the origin).

Symmetry

A matrix $A$ is symmetric if: $$ A^\top = A $$ For any quadratic form $x^\top Ax$, the asymmetric part of the matrix $A$ cancels itself out.

Any non-symmetric matrix can be split into a symmetric part and an anti-symmetric part. When a non-symmetric matrix is plugged into a quadratic form, the symmetric part determines the value. The anti-symmetric part always results in zero.

Because the non-symmetric parts do nothing to the shape of a function, to keep things simple, only symmetric matrices are used. It makes the math cleaner and ensures that all the eigenvalues are real numbers, which makes definiteness easy to see.
