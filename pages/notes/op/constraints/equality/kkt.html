KKT system formulation

$$ \begin{bmatrix} \nabla_{xx}^2 L & \nabla_x c \\ (\nabla_x c) ^\top & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\nabla_x L(x, \lambda) \\ -c(x) \end{bmatrix} $$ With regard to the left most matrix, there's a
  • Hessian in the top left.
  • Jacobian-transpose and a Jacobian of the constraints in the top and bottom right respectively.
  • 0 in the bottom right.
This structure is ubiquitous in optimisation. In constrained optimisation, problems are going to end up as a linear system as above. This linear system is called a KKT system based on the KKT conditions.

There are specialised solvers for it. There is even more structure lurking underneath that is exploited to solve it quickly.
