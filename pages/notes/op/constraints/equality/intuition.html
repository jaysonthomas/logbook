Intuition

Setup

The circles are the level curves of the objective function to be minimised. The blue line $c(x) = 0$ is the constraint.

$$\begin{align*} f(x) &: \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ c(x) &: \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \end{align*}$$

Gradient direction

In multivariable calculus and optimisation, the direction the gradient points is determined by two fundamental mathematical rules:
  • The Gradient is Orthogonal to Level Sets
    • The blue line labeled $c(x) = 0$ is a level set (or contour line) of the function $c$. The gradient vector $\nabla{c}(x)$ is always perpendicular (orthogonal) to the level set at any given point.

  • The Direction of Steepest Ascent
    • The gradient always points in the direction of the greatest increase of the function. Since the values of $c(x)$ increases towards the top-left, the gradient vector $\nabla{c}(x)$ points that way.

Free direction

Free direction refers to movements along the constraint line $c(x) = 0$. Rules of the constraint are not broken along this line.

At an optimal point, movement along the blue line shouldn't change the value of the objective function at all. This means the directional derivative of $f$ in the direction of the blue line must be zero. For the directional derivative to be zero, the gradient $\nabla f$ must be perpendicular to the direction of movement.
