KKT conditions

Based on the necessary conditions, the following mathematical packaging is defined purely for convenience. $$ L(x, \lambda) = f(x) + \lambda^\top c(x) $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} L(x, \lambda) &:& \text{Lagrangian. Scalar valued function.} \\ x &:& \text{Primal vector} \\ \lambda &:& \text{Dual vector or variable} \\ f(x) &:& \text{Function to be minimised} \\ \end{array}$$
The above convenience-formulation permits the definition of the following root-finding problem, where at the optimal point, the gradient of the Lagrangian is 0. $$ \nabla L(x, \lambda) = 0 $$ This conveniently expands into the 2 necessary conditions. $$\begin{align*} \nabla_x L(x, \lambda) &= \nabla_x{f} + \left(\frac{\partial{c}}{\partial{x}}\right)^\top \lambda = 0 \\ \nabla_\lambda L(x, \lambda) &= c(x) = 0 \end{align*}$$ These gradient conditions are called KKT conditions.

Derivatives

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial \lambda}(\nabla_x L) = \nabla_x c $$ $\nabla_x c$ is the transpose of the constraint jacobian.
