QP formulation

Aim

The aim is to define/rewrite the LQR problem as a QP problem. $$\begin{align*} \min_z \quad & \frac{1}{2} z^\top H z \\ s.t. \quad & Cz = d \end{align*}$$ This is an equality constrained QP.

Decision variables

Assume initial state $x_1$ is given. Hence, it's not a decision variable since it's not being optimised.

Define $z$ as a stack of all the decision variables in the LQR problem over the time horizon. $$ z = \begin{bmatrix} u_1 \\ x_2 \\ u_2 \\ x_3 \\ u_3 \\ \vdots \\ x_N \end{bmatrix} $$

Cost function

$$ H = \begin{bmatrix} R_1 & & & \huge 0 \\ & Q_2 & & \\ & & R_2 & \\ & & & \ddots \\ \huge 0 & & & & Q_N \end{bmatrix} $$ This is a block diagonal matrix, i.e. the off-diagonal blocks are 0 matrices.

The overall cost function becomes: $$ J = \frac{1}{2} z^\top H z $$

Dynamics constraints

They are a set of constraints, one for each time step. They need to be defined in terms of $z$. $x_1$ needs to show up in the constraints, but not in $z$. $$ \underbrace{ \begin{bmatrix} B_1 & -I & 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & A_2 & B_2 & -I & 0 & \vdots \\ & & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ & & & A_{N-1} & B_{N-1} & -I \end{bmatrix}}_{C} \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} u_1 \\ x_2 \\ u_2 \\ x_3 \\ u_3 \\ \vdots \\ x_N \end{bmatrix}}_{z} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -A_1 x_1 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{d} $$ The first 2 lines expand to: $$\begin{align*} B_1 u_1 - x_2 &= -A_1 x_1 \\ A_2 x_2 + B_2 u_2 - x_3 &= 0 \end{align*}$$ In the first line, $x_1$ is not part of the decision variables, hence it is moved to the RHS.
