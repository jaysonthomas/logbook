KKT system

Lagrangian

Lagrangian of the QP is: $$ L(z, \lambda) = \frac{1}{2} z^\top H z + \lambda^\top [Cz - d] $$ The lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ is for the dynamics constraints.

KKT conditions

Setting the gradients of the Lagrangian to 0. $$\begin{alignat*}{2} \nabla_z L &= Hz + C^\top \lambda &= 0 \\ \nabla_\lambda L &= Cz - d &= 0 \end{alignat*}$$

KKT linear system formulation

$$ \begin{bmatrix} H & C^\top \\ C & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} z \\ \lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ d \end{bmatrix} $$ Since no linearisation was required, reformulating the problem as a QP and then stacking as a linear system, Newton's method could solve this in a single iteration.
