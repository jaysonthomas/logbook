Setup

Overview

The aim is to simulate a discrete-time LQR controlling a simple 1D motion system (position + velocity), and publish the trajectory over time. The control input $u$ is acceleration.

State

$$ \begin{bmatrix} \text{p} \\ \text{v} \end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} p &:& \text{Position} \\ v &:& \text{Velocity} \end{array}$$
The system starts displaced from the origin. The controller's job is to drive the state to 0. $$x_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$$

State update

The next state is computed from the current state and control input. $$ x_{k+1} = Ax_k + Bu_k $$

Control input

The optimal feedback gain $K$ is computed using LQR. The optimal control law is: $$\begin{align*} u_k &= -K \; x_k \\ &= - \left(k_1 p_k + k_2 v_k \right) \\\\ K &= \begin{bmatrix} k_1 & k_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}$$ The controller uses both position error and velocity to determine acceleration. If position is positive, controller usually applies negative acceleration to bring it back.

State space model

Dynamics

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & h \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ This corresponds to: $$\begin{align*} p_{k+1} &= p_k + hv_k \\ v_{k+1} &= v_k \\ \end{align*}$$ Without control, velocity stays constant.

Control input

$$ B = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2}h^2 \\ h \end{bmatrix} $$ This says acceleration $u$ affects:
  • position through $\frac{1}{2}h^2$
  • velocity through $hu$

Cost matrices

LQR minimises the cost: $$ J = \sum_{k=0}^\infty x_k^\top Q_k x_k + u_k^\top R_k u_k $$ $$ Q = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ R = 0.1 $$

Interpretation

  • Large state values are penalised.
  • Since control effort is not penalised as much, the controller can act aggressively.

Simulation parameters

$$\begin{array}{ccl} h = 0.1 &:& \text{Time step} \\ t_f = 10 &:& \text{Total simulation time} \\ N = \frac{t_f}{h} + 1 &:& \text{Number of simulation steps} \end{array}$$
