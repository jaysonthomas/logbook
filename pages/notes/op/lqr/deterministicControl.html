Deterministic optimal control

Overview

The aim is to minimise a cost function $J$ w.r.t. state $x(t)$ and control $u(t)$ trajectories. $J$ is a function of functions since the state and control are functions of time. Considering the discrete version where discrete samples replace continuous time.

This is a finite time problem where once the end is reached at the final time step, there are no more control moves and a final cost is incurred.

$$ \min_{\substack{x_{1:N} \\ u_{1:N-1}}} \quad J(x_{1:N}, u_{1:N-1}) = \sum^{N-1}_{k=1} l(x_k, u_k) + l_f(x_N) \\ $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} l(x_k, u_k) &:& \text{Stage cost. Cost incurred at each time step.} \\ && \text{This is added along the trajectory.} \\ l_f(x_N) &:& \text{Terminal cost, a function of just the final state (the final landing spot)} \\ && \text{since there are no more moves to perform.} \\ u_{1:N-1} &:& \text{No control action is taken at the terminal state. } \\ && \text{Hence, one less control action than states.} \\ \end{array}$$

Solution

Samples $x_k, u_k$ are often called knot points since often splines are used to interpolate between points. Often the optimisation is solved for course knot points (say 5Hz), but then upscaled (500Hz) by interpolating using cubic splines between the solutions which leads to very smooth outputs.

The solutions are open loop trajectories in $(x, u)$. In a stochastic setting, the solutions are feedback policies since everything is noisy and some feedback is needed for stabilisation. RL can only provide feedback policies as solutions.

Aside from LQR, there are no other problems of this nature that have analytical solutions. In the latter case, numerical methods/optimisations are used.

Constrained optimisation problem

Constraints are enforced at each time step. They are usually evaluated for the knot points and not for the finer interpolated points due to the higher computational expense.

Following are different types of constraints:

Dynamics constraints

They are a set of equality constraints that are enforced at every time step. RK4 could be used to obtain the below for example. $$ s.t. \quad x_{k+1} = f(x_k, u_k) $$

Actuator constraints

These are bounds on $u$, torque limits, which are inequality constraints. $$ u_{min} \leq u_k \leq u_{max} \quad \forall k $$

Collision avoidance constraints

These are Obstacle/safety constraints which are state inequality constraints. For e.g. distance from the minimum radius around an object needs to be above some threshold. $$ c(x_k) \geq 0 \quad \forall k $$

Continuous time formulation

$$ \min_{\substack{x(t) \\ u(t)}} \quad J(x(t), u(t)) = \int_{t_0}^{t_f} l(x(t), u(t)) \; dt + l_f(x(t_f)) $$
