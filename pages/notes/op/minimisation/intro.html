Overview

Minimisation gets reformulated into root finding. Constrained minimisation gets reformulated into regular minimization with constraints and then finally into root finding. The objective function changes, but minimisation is basically a root-finding problem.

Math

$$ \min_x \, f(x) \quad f(x): \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R} $$ $f$ is a scalar valued function. It takes in a vector and output a scalar. The aim is to find the $x$ that gives the smallest return value/output of the function.

If $f$ is smooth, then at a minimum the function has to be flat; at a minimum the bottom is flat.

$$ \frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x}}\bigg|_{x^*} = 0 $$ At a local minimum $x^*$, the Jacobian must be 0. If the bottom is tilted then it means the function can go further down.
