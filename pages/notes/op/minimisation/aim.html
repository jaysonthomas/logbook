Aim

Minimisation gets reformulated into root finding. Constrained minimisation gets reformulated into regular minimisation with constraints and then finally into root finding.

Consider that $g$ is a scalar valued function - it takes in a vector and outputs a scalar. The aim is to find the $x$ that gives the smallest return value/output of the function. $$ \min_x \, g(x) \quad g(x): \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R} $$

Minimisation involves root finding on the gradient. The objective function: $$ g(x) = \nabla{f(x)} $$
