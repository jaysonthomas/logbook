Log domain interior point method

Overview

Consider the general case. $$\begin{align*} \min_x \quad & f(x) \\ s.t. \quad & c(x) \geq 0 \end{align*}$$ Simplify by introducing a slack variable (just a new variable that does not change the problem), $s$. The aim is not to have $c(x) \geq 0$ within a barrier.

Optimising over $x$ and $s$: $$\begin{align*} \min_{x,s} \quad & f(x) \\ s.t. \quad & c(x) - s = 0 \\ & s \geq 0 \end{align*}$$

$s$ is going to be inside the log barrier and via the equality constraint, $c$ is enforced that it is positive. This is just a mathematical trick that makes it nicer to work with.

This is converted into an equality constrained problem. $$\begin{align*} \min_{x,s} \quad & f(x) - \rho \; log(s) \\ s.t. \quad & c(x) - s = 0 \\ \end{align*}$$

Computing the Lagrangian: $$ L(x, s, \lambda) = f(x) - \rho \; log(s) - \lambda^\top \left(c(x) - s\right) $$

FON conditions

Setting the gradients to 0. $$\begin{align*} \nabla_x L &= \nabla f - \left(\frac{\partial c}{\partial x}\right)^\top \lambda = 0 \\ \nabla_s L &= -\frac{\rho}{s} + \lambda = 0 \\ \nabla_\lambda L &= c(x) - s = 0 \end{align*}$$ The gradient w.r.t. $\lambda$ just returns the constraints.

The gradient w.r.t. $s$ can be rewritten as the relaxed complementarity condition: $$ s \odot \lambda = \rho $$

To ensure $s \geq 0$ and $\lambda \geq 0$ (historically done using backtracking line search), introduce the following change of variables that takes advantage of the complimentarity slackness: $$ s = \sqrt{\rho} \; e^\sigma $$ Because of the above definition and the relaxed complementarity condition, $\lambda$ has to be: $$ \lambda = \sqrt{\rho} \; e^{-\sigma} $$ The complementarity condition in terms of $\sigma$ and the change of variables is satisfied by construction always. $s$ and $\lambda$ are also always guaranteed to be positive.

Simplifying the FON conditions by plugging the above into them yields the following equality-constrained problem which is a well-behaved, smooth function in $x$ and $\sigma$:

$$\begin{align*} \nabla f - \left(\frac{\partial c}{\partial x}\right)^\top \sqrt{\rho} \; e^{-\sigma} &= 0 \\ c(x) - \sqrt{\rho} \; e^\sigma &= 0 \end{align*}$$

Root finding

Consider the above objective function (a grouping of the 2). Applying a first order taylor series expansion: $$\begin{equation} f(x + \Delta x, \sigma + \Delta\sigma) \approx f(x, \sigma) + J(x, \sigma) \; \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta\sigma \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}$$ $f(x, \sigma)$ is a reformulation of $\Delta L(x, s, \lambda)$. To satisfy the FON conditions, $\Delta L$ needs to be 0. Hence, the above equation becomes: $$\begin{align} f(x, \sigma) + J(x, \sigma) \; \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta\sigma \end{bmatrix} &= 0 \nonumber \\ J(x, \sigma) \; \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta\sigma \end{bmatrix} &= -f(x, \sigma) \nonumber \\ Jd &= -r \label{eq:2} \end{align}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} -r &:& \text{Negative residual. Error. Any value that is not 0 is an error.} \\ && \text{It is the value of the function at the current guess.} \\ && \text{The negative sign indicates movement in the direction that cancels out the error.} \\ d &:& \text{Step. This is the vector of updates that is being solved for.} \\ J &:& \text{A matrix of slopes describing how every equation in the system changes w.r.t. every variable.} \end{array}$$

Jacobian

$$J = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial \sigma} \\ \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial \sigma} \end{bmatrix}$$

$\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x}$

The derivative of the gradient $\nabla f$ is the Hessian $H$. Gauss-Newton ignores the second-order derivatives of the constraints, so this simplifies to $H$.

$\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial \sigma}$

The derivative of $e^{-\sigma}$ is $-e^{-\sigma}$.

$\frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x}$

The derivative of $c(x)$ is simply the Jacobian of the constraints, denoted here as $c$.$\sqrt{\rho} e^\sigma$ is constant with respect to $x$.

KKT system

\ref{eq:2} becomes $$ \begin{bmatrix} H & \sqrt\rho c^\top e^{-\sigma} \\ c & -\sqrt\rho e^\sigma \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \sigma \end{bmatrix} = - \begin{bmatrix} \nabla f - c^\top \sqrt\rho e^{-\sigma} \\ c(x) - \sqrt\rho e^\sigma \end{bmatrix} $$

Example

A quadratic program is one with a quadratic objective as below where in order for it to be convex, Q has to be positive definite. $$\begin{align*} \min_x \quad & \frac{1}{2} x^\top Q x + q^\top x \qquad Q > 0 \\ s.t. \quad & Ax \geq b \\ & Cx = d \end{align*}$$ There are linear equality as well as inequality constraints.
