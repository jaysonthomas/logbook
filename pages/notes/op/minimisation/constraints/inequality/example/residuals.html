Residual functions

Log domain interior point method

From the log domain interior point method KKT system, the residual is: $$ \begin{bmatrix} \nabla f - c^\top \sqrt\rho e^{-\sigma} \\ c(x) - \sqrt\rho e^\sigma \end{bmatrix} $$

True KKT residual

$$r(z) = \begin{bmatrix} \nabla f(x) - \nabla c(x)^\top \lambda \\ \min(\lambda, 0) \\ \min(c(x), 0) \\ \lambda c(x) \end{bmatrix}$$ Where $\lambda = \sqrt{\rho} \; e^{-\sigma}$
  • Stationarity: The first term checks if the gradients are parallel.

  • Complementary Slackness: $\lambda c(x)$ ensures that either the constraint is active ($c(x) = 0$) or the multiplier is zero ($\lambda = 0$).

Redundant checks:

  • Dual Feasibility: $\min(\lambda, 0)$ checks if the multiplier has violated the non-negativity constraint $\lambda \geq 0$. (Note: Because $\lambda = e^{-\sigma}$, this term is mathematically guaranteed to be $0$ unless there is numerical underflow).

  • Primal Feasibility: $\min(c(x), 0)$ checks if the inequality constraint $c(x) \geq 0$ is satisfied.
