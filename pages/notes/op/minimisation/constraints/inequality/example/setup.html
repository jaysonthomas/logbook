Objective function

$$\begin{align*} Q_{n \times n} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \\ K_{n \times 1} &= x - a \\ a &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \\ f(x) &= \frac{1}{2} K^\top_{1 \times n} \cdot Q \cdot K \\ \end{align*}$$

Jacobian

The Jacobian of a scalar function is defined as the column vector of its first partial derivatives. This follows the jacobian notation.

For a quadratic function of the form $f(x) = \frac{1}{2} (x-a)^\top Q (x-a)$ where $Q$ is symmetric, the jacobian is: $$ \nabla_x f(x)_{n \times 1} = Q(x - a) $$

Hessian

$$ \nabla^2_x f(x)_{n \times n} = Q $$

Constraint function

$$ c(x) = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{A} \cdot x - \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{b} $$

Gradient

$$ \frac{\partial c(x)}{\partial x} = A $$
