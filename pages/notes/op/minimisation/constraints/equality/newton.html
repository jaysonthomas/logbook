Applying Newton's method

The Newton step

Newton's method finds the root of a function by using a first-order Taylor expansion of the gradient. The aim is to find a step $(\Delta x, \Delta \lambda)$ such that: $$ \nabla L(x + \Delta x, \lambda + \Delta \lambda) = 0 $$ Expanding $\nabla L$ around the current point $(x, \lambda)$ using the multivariable Taylor series: $$ \nabla L(x + \Delta x, \lambda + \Delta \lambda) \approx \nabla L(x, \lambda) + \mathbf{H}_L(x, \lambda) \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \lambda \end{bmatrix} = 0 $$ Where $\mathbf{H}_L$ is the Hessian of the Lagrangian.

Expanding the partial gradients

The gradient $\nabla L$ is a vector composed of partial derivatives with respect to $x$ and $\lambda$ $$ \nabla L = \begin{bmatrix} \nabla_x L \\ \nabla_\lambda L \end{bmatrix} $$

W.r.t. $x$

Expanding the top row, the stationarity condition for $x$ and substituting partial derivative: $$\begin{align*} \nabla_x L(x + \Delta x, \lambda + \Delta \lambda) &\approx \nabla_x L(x, \lambda) + \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(\nabla_x L) \Delta x + \frac{\partial}{\partial \lambda}(\nabla_x L) \Delta \lambda \\ &\approx \nabla_x L(x, \lambda) + \nabla_{xx}^2 L \; \Delta{x} + \nabla_x c \; \Delta \lambda \\ &= 0 \end{align*}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} \nabla_x L(x, \lambda) &:& \text{Current gradient} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(\nabla_x L) &:& \frac{\partial^2 L}{\partial{x}^2} \text{, often written as } \nabla_{xx}^2 L \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial \lambda}(\nabla_x L) &:& \frac{\partial^2 L}{\partial{x}\,\partial\lambda} \end{array}$$

W.r.t. $\lambda$

$$\begin{align*} \nabla_\lambda L(x + \Delta x, \lambda + \Delta \lambda) &\approx c(x) + \frac{\partial c}{\partial x} \Delta x \\ &= 0 \end{align*}$$
