Gauss-Newton method

$$ \frac{\partial^2 L}{\partial{x}^2} = \nabla^2 f + \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[\nabla_x c \, \lambda \right] $$ The 2nd term on the RHS is called the constraint curvature term since it is a 2nd derivative of the constraints. $c$ is a vector. $\nabla_x c$ is a matrix. Derivative of that gives a 3rd rank tensor. Hence why it is expensive to compute. Sophisticated auto-diffing tools can be used.

The term is often dropped to use only the Hessian of the objective (instead of the Hessian of the Lagrangian). When dropped, it is called Gauss Newton method. It is a standard method used in robotics applications.

The Gauss Newton method is slower to converge than full Newton. It requires more iterations, but iterations are cheaper to compute. It often wins in wall-clock time.
