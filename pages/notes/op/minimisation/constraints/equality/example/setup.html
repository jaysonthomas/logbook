Jacobian

The Jacobian of a scalar function is defined as the column vector of its first partial derivatives. This follows the jacobian notation.

For a quadratic function of the form $f(x) = \frac{1}{2} (x-a)^\top Q (x-a)$ where $Q$ is symmetric, the jacobian is: $$ \nabla_x f(x)_{n \times 1} = Q(x - a) $$

Hessian

$$ \nabla^2_x f(x)_{n \times n} = Q $$
