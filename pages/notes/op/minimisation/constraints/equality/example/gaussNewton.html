Gauss Newton step

Overview

See Lagrangian: $$ \nabla_x L(x, \lambda) = \nabla_x{f} + \nabla_x c \lambda $$ Using the gauss-newton simplification, the KKT formulation becomes: $$ \begin{bmatrix} \nabla^2_x f(x) & \nabla_x c(x) \\ (\nabla_x c(x)) ^\top & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\nabla_x f(x) - \nabla_x c(x) \lambda \\ -c(x) \end{bmatrix} $$ This can be rearranged to find the step change $$\begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \lambda \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} \nabla^2_x f(x) & \nabla_x c(x) \\ (\nabla_x c(x)) ^\top & 0 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} -\nabla_x f(x) - \nabla_x c(x) \lambda \\ -c(x) \end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} (n \times n) & (n \times 1) \\ (1 \times n) & 0 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} (n \times 1) - (n \times 1) \lambda \\ -c(x) \end{bmatrix}_{(n+1 \times 1)} \\ \end{align*}$$ Refer Julia's backslash operator.
