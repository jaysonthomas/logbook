KKT system formulation

Newton's step can be written in matrix notation as follows: $$ \begin{bmatrix} \nabla_{xx}^2 L & \nabla_x c \\ (\nabla_x c) ^\top & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \Delta x \\ \Delta \lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\nabla_x L(x, \lambda) \\ -c(x) \end{bmatrix} $$ With regard to the left most matrix, there's a
  • Hessian in the top left.
  • Jacobian and a Jacobian-transpose of the constraints in the top-right and bottom-left respectively.
  • 0 in the bottom right.
This structure is ubiquitous in optimisation. In constrained optimisation, problems are going to end up as a linear system as above. This linear system is called a KKT system based on the KKT conditions.

There are specialised solvers for it. There is even more structure lurking underneath that is exploited to solve it quickly.

Solving this system involves simultaneously trying to minimize the objective function (via the Hessian and gradient) while moving toward a point where the constraints $c(x) = 0$ are satisfied.
