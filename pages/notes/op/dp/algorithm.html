Algorithm

Steps

Set the cost-to-go $V$ at the final state to the terminal cost: $$ V_N(x) = l_N(x) $$ Set time step $k = N$. while k > 1

Inequality constraints can be applied on the torque $u$ by drawing it from a set $U$ that has the constraints. Applying a min over just 1 step:

$$ V_{k-1}(x) = \min_{u \in \mathcal{U}} [l(x, u) + V_k(f(x, u))] $$ Continuing backwards by setting time step $k = k-1$. $V_k(x)$ is now known $\forall k$. The optimal policy is therefore: $$ u_k(x) = \arg \min_{u \in \mathcal{U}} \; [l(x, u) + V_{k+1}(f(x, u))] $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} l(x, u) &:& \text{One step cost} \\ V_{k+1} &:& \text{Cost-to-go} \end{array}$$
