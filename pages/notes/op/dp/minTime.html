DP: Minimum Time Problem

Overview

Refer to double integrator plant here.

Goal

The goal is to apply a force $u$ to move a mass $m=1$ over a frictionless surface, to the origin, where its position $q$ and velocity needs to be $0$.

In other words:

  • To find a control action $u$ that drives the system to stop at the origin in as little time as possible or
  • To design some feedback law (control policy, $\pi$) which is a function of $x$: $$ u = \pi(x) $$
  • To find the cost/reward function or value function or cost-to-go function

Restriction

$|u| \leq 1$

Solution

The Optimal solution

Go as fast as possible. At the last possible second, slam on the brakes and skid into the parking spot. This is with a stopped initial condition. It's called a bang-bang control where the limits are slammed at all times. It's a non-smooth controller. There could be an initial condition where the brakes are hit immediately.

Generalisation of the bang-bang control: no matter what the initial condition of $x$, the decision needs to be made whether to accelerate or decelerate.

Finding the solution

How can bang-bang control be obtained from an algorithm? Minimum time as an optimization problem is closely related to finding the shortest path in computer science.

Discrete representation of a system's dynamics

A system's dynamics can be approximated with grid points, from each of which, a small number of edges, that represent different control decisions (like u=+1,-1,0), can be taken. The discrete grid point is just an approximation of a continuous state in the discrete grid - it won't be exactly equal to the equivalent continuous state.
