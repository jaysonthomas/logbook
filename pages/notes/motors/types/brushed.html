Brushed DC motor

Overview

[Ref]

The electromagnet forms the rotor. If the north pole of the stator is on the left, ideally the north pole of the rotor is also on the left. This would create a repulsion causing the electromagnet to rotate. After half a rotation, the south pole now moves to the left. In order to switch the poles again, the direction of the current flow needs to be reversed. This ensures the north pole of the electromagnet is always on the left.

In order to avoid manual switching of the current direction, a commutator along with spring loaded carbon brushes are used.
