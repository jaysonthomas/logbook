Prediction step

Overview

The dynamics model is used to propagate the distribution forward by one time step. No measurements are included yet.

State

First, the state estimate or the mean of the distribution is predicted using the dynamics. $$\begin{aligned} \hat{x}_{k+1|k} &= E\left[x_{k+1} | y_{1:k}\right] \\ &= E\left[ A x_k + B u_k + w_k \mid y_{1:k} \right] \\ &= A \hat{x}_{k|k} + B u_k \end{aligned}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} \hat{x}_{k+1|k} &:& \text{The subscript means it's the state estimate at $k+1$ given measurements up to time $k$.} \\ w_k &:& \text{Stochastic dynamics.} \end{array}$$
$u_k$ is known and is not a random variable. A zero mean Gausian noise is assumed, hence $E[w] = 0$. This results in the final equation where the dynamics is applied to the last best state estimate.

Covariance

$$\begin{aligned} \Sigma_{k+1|k} &= E\left[ (x_{k+1} - \hat{x}_{k+1|k}) (\cdots)^T \right] \\ &= E\left[ (A x_k + \cancel{B u_k} + w_k - A \hat{x}_{k|k} - \cancel{B u_k}) (\cdots)^T \right] \\ &= A \underbrace{E\left[ (x_k - \hat{x}_{k|k}) (\cdots)^T \right]}_{\Sigma_{k|k}} A^T + \underbrace{E\left[ w_k w_k^T \right]}_{W} \\ &= A \Sigma_{k|k} A^T + W \end{aligned}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} W &:& \text{Process covariance} \end{array}$$
$A$ and $E[.]$ are linear, so $A$ can be taken outside. $x_k$ and $w_k$ are uncorrelated; hence the product is 0.

Intuition

The end equation passes the state covariance through the dynamics and adds on the process noise to make the noise bigger.

$A \Sigma_{k|k} A^T$ is how a coariance matrix is propagated through a linear transformation. A change of coordinates takes place on the matrix. It shows up all the time where a linear change of coordinates is applied to a matrix that is in a different coordinate system.
