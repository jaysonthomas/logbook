Innovation

Definition

It is the error in the prediction - difference between the actual and the predicted measurement (computed based on the predicted state). This error is linearly fedback in the kalman filter. $$\begin{aligned} z_{k+1} &= y_{k+1} - C\hat{x}_{k+1|k} \\ &= Cx_{k+1} + v_{k+1} - C\hat{x}_{k+1|k} \end{aligned}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} y &:& \text{Actual measurement} \\ C &:& \text{Measurement model} \\ v &:& \text{Measurement noise} \\ y_k = Cx_k + v_k &:& \text{Measurement equation} \end{array}$$
A Gaussian stays Gaussian under linear affine transformations (e.g. linear dynamics or linear measurement model) which is what makes the KF derivation work.

$A,B,C$ are assumed to be LTI. But, the same applies for the time-varying case.

Innovation Covariance

$$\begin{aligned} S_{k+1} &= E[z_{k+1} z_{k+1}^\top] \\ &= E[(Cx_{k+1} + v_{k+1} - C\hat{x}_{k+1|k})(\cdots)^\top] \end{aligned}$$ The state $x_{k+1}$ and the sensor noise $v_{k+1}$ are assumed to be uncorrelated. They can be correlated; for e.g. IMU readings vary with extreme temperature and can affect state estimation. $$\begin{align*} S_{k+1} &= C \underbrace{E \left[ (x_{k+1} - \hat{x}_{k+1|k})(\dots)^T \right]}_{\Sigma_{k+1|k}} C^T + \underbrace{E \left[ v_{k+1} v_{k+1}^T \right]}_{V} \\ &= C \Sigma_{k+1|k} C^T + V \end{align*}$$ Innovation is the error signal that is fed back into an estimator.
