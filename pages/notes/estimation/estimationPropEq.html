RLS: Estimation propagation equation

The aim of RLS is to compute the recursive estimate in the following form (time propagation of the estimate or the estimate propagation equation): $$\begin{equation} \label{eq:estimatePropagation} \hat{x}_k = \hat{x}_{k-1} + K_k(y_k - H_k \hat{x}_{k-1}) \end{equation}$$

State/Parameter vector

$x$ is the unknown state or parameter vector to be estimated.

If there are multiple terms in $x = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}$, then estimate $\hat{x}_k$ is partitioned as $\hat{x}_k = \begin{bmatrix} \hat{x}_{1,k} \\ \vdots \\ \hat{x}_{n,k} \end{bmatrix}$

Measurement model

$y_k$, the measurement sampled at discrete-time instant $k$, is related to $x$ by the following measurement equation/model: $$\begin{equation} \label{eq:measurementModel} y_k = H_kx + v_k \end{equation}$$

Measurement noise vector

The covariance and mean of the measurement noise vector $v_k$ is assumed to be: $$\begin{align*} E[v_kv^T_k] &= R_k \\ E[v_k] &= 0 \end{align*}$$
