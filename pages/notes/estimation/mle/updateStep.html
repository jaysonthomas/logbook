Update step for a gaussian distribution

Maximising the cost function (by setting its derivative to $0$) and solving for $x$ yields the equation for the maximum likelihood estimator $\hat{x}$ [Derivation]: $$ \hat{x} = (H^TR^{-1}H)^{-1}H^TR^{-1}y $$ This is the most likely estimate for a gaussian distribution. The cost function would be different for any other distribution (since the PDF would be different) yielding a different estimator equation.

This solution happens to be the same as the (batch) least squares estimation update equation where measurements $y$ are linearly related to some unknown quantity $x$ by the matrix $H$ that is assumed to be known.
