Measurement setup

Measurement model

Measurement model $h$ takes a state as input and returns the measurement expected to be seen from a perfect sensor for that state.

Consider a 1D drone having a range sensor pointing downwards, which measures its distance to the ground.

$$\begin{align*} h_t = h(x_t) &= [x_{t,z}] \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \dot{z} \\ z\end{bmatrix} \\ &= C_t \begin{bmatrix} \dot{z} \\ z\end{bmatrix} \\ &= C_t \; x_t \end{align*}$$

Jacobian of the measurement model

For a linear measurement model, the Jacobian is just the $C$ matrix. $$ H_t = h'(x_t) = C_t $$
