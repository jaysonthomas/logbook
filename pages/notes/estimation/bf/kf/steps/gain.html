Kalman gain

Aim

Kalman gain $K$ helps decide how much a new measurement is trusted compared to the belief after the prediction step.

Inputs

$K$ is computed:
  • Using the Jacobian of the measurement model and
  • indirectly, the Jacobian of the state transition function which helps compute the estimate of the state covariance matrix.
$$\begin{align*} H_t &= h'_t(\bar{\mu}_t) \\ K_t &= \bar{\Sigma}_t \; H^T_t \; (H_t \bar{\Sigma}_t H^T_t + R_t)^{-1} \end{align*}$$

Steps

The Kalman gain calculation involves the following 3 steps:
  • Inside the bracket, the estimated covariance is transformed into the measurement space using $H$.

  • Measurement noise $R$ is added to it.
    • There is uncertainty associated with sensor measurements due to sensor inaccuracy, which is captured by the covariance matrix $R$.

  • (Outside the bracket) The result is then transformed back into the state space to get the Kalman gain.
