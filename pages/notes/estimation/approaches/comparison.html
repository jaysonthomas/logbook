Comparison

Overview

The different approaches are just different ways of answering the same question: "Based on the data, what is the best guess for the underlying state?"

If the data follows a normal distribution, the math for MLE and LS becomes exactly the same, i.e., minimising the distance is the same as maximising the probability.

Least squares

Goal

Minimise the distance between the measured and predicted data points, referred to as errors or residuals.

Logic

If the distance between each measured/predicted point is squared, and then the sum of those squares is minimised, then the most optimal solution can be found that sits in the middle of the 2 sets of points.

Intuition

It is a geometric approach; it purely cares about spatial fit. There is no need to know why the data is messy; the aim is to just be as close to all the points as possible.

MLE

Goal

Maximise the likelihood function.

Logic

A specific distribution (like a Bell Curve) is assumed. The parameters of that curve (the mean and spread) is then tweaked until the "peak" of the curve aligns best with the measured data.

Intuition

MLE is more like a detective. Instead of looking at distances, it takes a probabilistic approach. It asks: "Out of all the possible versions of the world, which one matches the measured data the most?"
