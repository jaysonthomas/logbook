Estimation approaches: Introduction

Overview

Each estimation approach makes slightly different assumptions about various components of the measurement equation. In its simplest form, if a measurement equation is linear, it will look like: $$ y = Hx + v $$
  • Observations or measurements $y$

  • Jacobian or measurement model $H$
    • The measurement could be a linear or non-linear function of the true state.

  • Noise model $v$
    • Could be a gaussian distribution or not.

  • State vector $x$
    • Constant
    • Non-constant
