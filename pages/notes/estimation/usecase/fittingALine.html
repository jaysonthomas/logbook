Estimation usecase: Fitting A Line With Linear LS

Setup: state to be estimated

Consider data sampled from a straight line (a linear function) $y = at + b$ with some added unknown noise. The aim is to find the properties of the line which form the unknown state vector $x$.

Measurement equation

The equation for the maximum likelihood estimate can be used to find the best estimate for x, $\hat{x}$, given the measurements and $H$ are known. $$\begin{align*} \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ \vdots \\ y_k \end{bmatrix}}_{y_{k \times 1}} &= \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} t_1 & 1 \\ \vdots & \vdots \\ t_k & 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{H_{k \times m}} \cdot \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \end{bmatrix}}_{x_{m \times 1}} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ \vdots \\ v_k \end{bmatrix}}_{v_{k \times 1}} \\ y_i &= at_i + b + v_i \end{align*}$$
