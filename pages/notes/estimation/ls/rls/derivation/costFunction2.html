Cost function

Standard form

Instead of minimising the error directly, LS uses a probabilistic formulation to minimise the expected value of the squared measurement residual i.e. the variance of the unknowns, at every time step. The lower the variance, the more certain the estimate becomes.

For RLS (since it's not batched), the cost function at time $k$:

  • For a single scalar parameter, is the variance
    • $$\begin{align*} \mathcal L_{RLS} &= \mathbb{E}[(x - \hat{x}_k)^2] \\ &= \sigma^2_k \end{align*}$$
  • For multiple $n$ unknown parameters, is the trace of the covariance matrix
    • $$\begin{align*} \mathcal L_{RLS} &= \mathbb{E}[(x_1 - \hat{x}_{1k})^2 + \cdots + (x_n - \hat{x}_{nk})^2] \\ &= Trace(\underbrace{P_k}_{\text Estimator \atop \text covariance}) \end{align*}$$

Alternate form

To find the likelihood $\hat{x}$ of a set of measurements, the PDF corresponding to the measurments needs to be maximised.
