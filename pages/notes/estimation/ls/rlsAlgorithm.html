RLS: Algorithm

Initialise the estimator

Initialise the estimate and the covariance matrix. The initial estimate could come from the first measurement $y_0$ and the covariance could come from technical specifications. $$\begin{align*} \hat{x}_0 &= \mathbb{E}[x] \\ P_0 &= \mathbb{E}[(x-\hat{x}_0)(x-\hat{x}_0)^T] \end{align*}$$

Set up the measurement model

Define the Jacobian and the measurement noise with some mean and covariance matrix $R$. $$ y_k = H_kx + v_k $$

Update the estimate and the covariance

For every measurement $y_k$, the measurement gain $K_k$ is computed and used to update the estimate and the estimator covariance or uncertainty $P_k$. $$\begin{align*} K_k &= P_{k-1}H^T_k(H_kP_{k-1}H^T_k + R_k)^{-1} \\ \hat{x}_k &= \hat{x}_{k-1} + K_k(y_k - H_k\hat{x}_{k-1}) \\ P_k &= (I-K_kH_k)P_{k-1} \end{align*}$$ Every time a new measurement is taken, the uncertainty should shrink.
