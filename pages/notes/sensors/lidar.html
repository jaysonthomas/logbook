LIDAR

Definition

Lidar is a sensor and stands for light detection and ranging. It sends out beams of light, and measures how long it takes for the beam of light to come back to determine the distance to an object.

A lidar is a contraption consisting of a laser, a method of deflection allowing the laser beam to be scanned across the field of view and a photo detector that detects the incoming photons that are reflected from the object. The light pulse sent out is of a very short duration of a few nanoseconds. For every point in the FOV we get a distance measurement (or none if there is no object).

Sensor fusion is the process of combining data from multiple sensors to better understand the world, thereby taking advantage of what different sensors are good at. For example, lidar is higher resolution than radar, but radar can directly measure velocity. Combining the 2, we can get high spatial understanding where an object is at and at the same time a good idea of how fast it is moving.
