Overview

A LIDAR (light detection and ranging) sends out beams of light, and measures how long it takes for a beam of light to come back to determine the distance to an object. Laser intensity value is also received and can be used to evaluate material properties of the object the laser reflects off of.

A lidar is a contraption consisting of a:

  • Laser
  • Method of deflection allowing the laser beam to be scanned across its field of view
  • Photo detector that detects the incoming photons that are reflected from an object
The light pulse sent out is of a very short duration of a few nanoseconds. For every point in the FOV, a distance measurement (or none if there is no object) is received.
