Fixing stud to bricks using bolts

Overview

With the coach bolts and their appropriate plugs, it doesn't actually matter the brick or the mortar joints in between bricks. A coach bolt will spread a plug out enough to bite into any surface. A brick fixing would just pull away if they are inserted into mortar. They are good for fitting a door or a window though.

Ref

  • The DIY guy

Steps

  • 70 mm length, coach bolt
  • Measure the head of the bolt, which needs to be buried in the wood.
  • Make a ~6 mm recess in the timber using a 25 mm flat bit. Take a little bit at a time. We need enough wood to still remain so that the coach bolt can tightly pull the stud towards the wall without splitting through.
  • Use 10 mm drill bit. Make sure to drill straight.
  • Use a tracer pencil to mark the holes to drill. Make sure the timber is plum.
