Overview of Li-ion battery

Most Li-ion batteries are built from 18650 or 21700 cells (each ~3.6 V nominal, ~4.2 V fully charged). To reach a higher voltage, manufacturers connect cells in series. A dual-voltage battery like the Erbauer is built with 10 cells in series (10S). What makes it “dual” is the ability to reconfigure how the tool taps into those cells.

18V mode

The 10 cells are treated as two parallel strings of 5 cells each. Capacity = doubled (since two strings are in parallel), so 6.0 Ah. Energy: $18 V \times 6.0 Ah ≈ 108 Wh$

Parallel mode: Lower voltage, higher current capacity.

36V mode

The same 10 cells are treated as one series string of 10 cells. Capacity = the same as a single string, so 3.0 Ah. Energy: $36 V \times 3.0 Ah ≈ 108 Wh$

Series mode: Higher voltage, lower current capacity.
