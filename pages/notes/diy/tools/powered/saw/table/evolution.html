Steps to run

  • Ensure the insert plate is level with the surface.
    • Use a small piece of wood to move across (left/right, forward/backwards) to see if it gets stuck if there is a height difference.

  • Ensure the blade is actually at $90^0$ and $45^0$.
    • Adjust the angle marker.

  • Ensure the blade is tracking correctly.
    • Mark a point on the blade. Meaasure between this point and a point on the mitre slot. Rotate the blade to the other end and do the same measurement.

  • Ensure the blade is square to the table and the mitre slots.

  • Ensure the riving knife is in line with the blade.

  • Ensure the blade is square to the fence
    • The distance between the 2 ends of the blade (near the surface) and the fence should be the same.

  • Ensure correct blade.
  • Ensure fence is in line with mitre slot, both on the silver and black pins.
  • Whilst on the black pin, ensure the closest the fence can get to the blade is 10 cm away.
  • Adjust the fence gauge.
