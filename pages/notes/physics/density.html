Density

Density is defined as the mass of a unit volume of a material. It is an intensive property of a material, which means that it does not depend on the amount of material present. For example, a block of gold with a mass of 100 grams and a volume of 5 cubic centimeters has the same density as a block of gold with a mass of 1 kilogram and a volume of 100 cubic centimeters. It can be used to identify materials and to determine the amount of material in a given volume.

Density measures the amount of mass per unit volume of a substance (kg/mÂ³). It considers the mass of a substance in relation to its volume, providing information on the compactness (or how spread out) or density of a substance.

This would be useful when trying to understand the overall mass distribution within the rod regardless of its length. Density provides information on how compact the material of the rod is, irrespective of its shape. It is useful when trying to understand material properties.

Linear density

Linear density $\frac{kg}{m}$ is the amount of mass per unit length along a linear dimension, such as a line, wire, or rod. It focuses solely on the distribution of mass along a linear dimension, disregarding the volume. This is useful when analysing properties like tension or stress along the length of a rod due to its own weight.
