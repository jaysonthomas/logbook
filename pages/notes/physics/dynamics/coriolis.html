Coriolis force

Overview

Consider a big spinning disk (like a merry-go-round) rotating with some angular velocity $\omega$. Imagine someone, standing in the middle of the disc, tried to throw a ball at the triangular target shown in the image.

Inertial frame of reference

Imagine looking from above.

The ball doesn't care that the disk is rotating and it moves in a straight line. By the time it gets to the edge of the disk, the target will have rotated far away from where it originally was. Here, the motion is observed from the world frame, which is an inertial frame. In this frame, there is no Coriolis Force.

Rotating frame of reference

Imagine standing on the triangular target and observing the motion of the ball. From this point of view (a rotating frame of reference), the ball would take a curved path. It would seem like the ball is being pushed sideways by some force, the Coriolis Force $F_c$.
