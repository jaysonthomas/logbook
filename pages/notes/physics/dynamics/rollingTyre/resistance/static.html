Static friction

Overview

The static friction that needs to be overcome to get a tyre rolling is not the tyre-road friction. If it were, putting stickier racing tires on a car would make it harder to push, which is not true.

Difference between rolling and sliding

Consider a block on a road.
  • If the block is sliding (like a sled), static friction is between the block and the road.
  • If the block is a car on wheels, static friction to overcome is internal to the wheels/axles. The road friction actually prevents the wheels from sliding so they can roll.

Sources

  • Axles, bearings, (transmission) gears
    • Even with lubrication, metal-on-metal parts settle when stopped. Extra force is needed to break the initial molecular bonds to get the oil film flowing again.

  • Brake Drag
    • Brake pads often rest ever-so-slightly against the rotors even when not braking.

  • Tyre Deformation
    • When a car sits, the tyres flatten slightly at the bottom. Overcoming this flat spot to get the first roll requires a spike of force (often grouped with static resistance).
