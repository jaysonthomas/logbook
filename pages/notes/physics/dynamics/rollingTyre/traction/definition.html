Definition

Overview

Traction force is the reaction force that actually propels a vehicle (or any object) forward. It is also called longitudinal friction force and acts in the direction of motion.
$$\begin{array}{ccl} F_m &:& \text{Applied motive force} \\ F &:& \text{Reaction traction force} \\ \tau_r &:& \text{Resistive torque} \\ \tau_m &:& \text{Motor torque} \\ \end{array}$$

Description

In normal driving (rolling), the aim is not to overcome friction force. But, to use it.
  • To move forward, a torque is applied to the wheel. This creates a force at the contact patch (where the molecules of rubber and road physically interact) that pushes backwards on the ground.
  • The ground pushes forward on the wheel with an equal and opposite force. This reaction force is called longitudinal friction force. Since it propels a vehicle forward, it is also called traction force.
