Names

Reaction force

A tyre pushes backward on a road. As per Newton's 3rd law, the road then pushes the tyre forward. The car cannot move itself; it needs an external object (the road) to push back against it. This name is given from a physics perspective.

Longitudinal Friction Force

This name is given from a tyre dynamics (physical mechanism creating the force) perspective. Through the mechanical interlocking (grip) between the rubber and the asphalt, a road is able to push a tyre. The name distinguishes it from lateral friction (cornering) or rolling resistance.

Traction force

This name is given from a functional (focuses on the useful result of the force) perspective. Traction describes the force's ability to do work - specifically, to pull or push a vehicle forward. The name distinguishes useful propulsion from other types of friction (like drag or braking).

Summary

$$\begin{align*} \text{Force from road} &= \text{Reaction force (origin)} \\ &= \text{Longitudinal friction force (mechanism)} \\ &= \text{Traction (function)} \end{align*}$$
