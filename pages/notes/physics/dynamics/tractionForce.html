Dynamics: Traction force

Overview

Traction force is the force that actually propels a vehicle (or any object) forward.
$$\begin{array}{ccl} F_s &:& \text{Static friction} \\ F_t &:& \text{Traction force} \\ \tau_r &:& \text{Resistive torque} \\ \tau_m &:& \text{Motor torque} \\ \end{array}$$

Acceleration

When a tire pushes against a road surface:
  • If static friction (due to the grip of the contact patch on the road) is not overcome, the tire does not slip (loss of grip). This allows a reaction force, traction force, to push the tire forward - it is the effective force driving the tire forward.

  • Traction force acts in the direction of motion.

  • If a tire begins to slip, the friction involved becomes kinetic friction, which acts opposite to the direction of sliding motion with a lower maximum value than static friction. The car then loses effective traction.

Deceleration

When braking, the aim is to allow a tire's contact patch with the road to have a longer period gripping onto each other. This allows the tire to slow down and eventually come to a halt. Traction force is still in the direction of motion.
