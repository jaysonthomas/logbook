Simple moving average filter

Difference equation

$$ y[n] = \frac{1}{N}\sum^{N-1}_{i=0} x[n-i] $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} y[n] &:& \text{Current output} \\ x[n] &:& \text{Current input} \\ x[n-1] &:& \text{Previous input and so on} \\ N &:& \text{Window size or length of the average} \\ \end{array}$$

Setup

A signal is being sampled at 16kHz. It is then sub-sampled at 200Hz. In order to minimise noise in the low-frequency signal, a moving average filter is to be implemented at high frequency.

Window size calculation

[Ref - see comments for details]

The cutoff frequency of a moving average filter: $$ f_c \approx \frac{0.443 \cdot f_s}{N} $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} f_s &:& \text{16kHz} \\ N &:& \text{Window size} \\ \end{array}$$
The sub-sampled nyquist frequency is 100Hz. The filter should attenuate frequency components above $f_c=100$ to avoid aliasing. The ideal window size in this case would be $N=~70.88$
