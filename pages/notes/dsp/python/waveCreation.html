Python: Wave creation

Overview


x = amplitude * np.sin(2 * np.pi * frequency * t)
Consider 2 cases:
  • A: $f=10$Hz, $t=0.5$s
  • B: $f=5$Hz, $t=1$s
At the 2 time instants, the value in the 2 cases are the same. But, since A's frequency is higher, it has attained the same value in a shorter period of time.

The general formula is: $$ a\cdot sin(2\pi ft + \phi) $$ $\phi$ is the phase shift in radians. $\phi > 0$ shifts the wave left (it starts earlier).
