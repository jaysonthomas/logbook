Description

The integral of a function is the area under the curve. The integral of an error over time, is a useful tool for accumulating error, to then adjust the system to counter this accumulation and reach a target as shown in the second image on the right below.

Anti-windup

[More on saturation]
Actuators are not linear systems - they have physical limitations and cannot entirely follow the commands given to them. Linear systems on the other hand have virtually no limitations and respond linearly to any command.

Anti-windup schemes like clamping, back-calculation, conditional integration, or dynamic feedback can prevent the integral term from accumulating when saturation occurs.
