Cost function: Derivative

Vector equations

From equations 69 and 81, from the Matrix cookbook: $$\begin{align*} \frac{\partial w^T a}{\partial w} &= \frac{\partial a^T w}{\partial w} = a \\ \frac{\partial w^T H w}{\partial w} &= 2Hw \end{align*}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} w &:& \text{A vector} \\ a &:& \text{A constant vector} \\ H &:& \text{A constant symmetric matrix} \end{array}$$

Derivative

Applying the above equations: $$\begin{align*} \frac{\partial{s^T \, W_P \,s}}{\partial{u}} &= 0 \\ \frac{\partial{s^T \, W_P \, Mu}}{\partial{u}} &= M^T \, W_P \, s \\ \frac{\partial{u^T \, M^T \, W_P \, s}}{\partial{u}} &= M^T \, W_P \, s \\ \frac{\partial{u^T \, M^T \, W_P \, Mu}}{\partial{u}} &= 2M^T \, W_P \, Mu \\ \frac{\partial{u^T W u}}{\partial{u}} &= 2Wu \end{align*}$$ The derivative of the cost function becomes: $$ \frac{\partial{J}}{\partial{u}} = -2M^T \, W_P \, s + 2M^T \, W_P \, Mu + 2Wu $$
