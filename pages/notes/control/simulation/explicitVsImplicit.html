Explicit vs implicit form

Explicit method

The state of a system at a future time step is calculated by explicitly plugging already known information. $$ y_{\text{new}} = f(y_{\text{old}}) $$ If the time step ($\Delta t$) is too large, the simulation explodes because it can't react to changes happening during the step. It is very fast to calculate; no complex algebra required per step.

Implicit method

The state of a system at a future time step is calculated using the future state. $$ y_{\text{new}} = f(y_{\text{old}}, y_{\text{new}}) $$ $y_{new}$ is on both sides of the equals sign. It cannot be simply calculated; It has to be solved for, often using algebraic manipulation or iterative solvers like Newton's Method. This is computationally expensive. Every single step requires solving an equation or a system of equations.

Even if massive time steps are taken, a simulation will not explode since it is self-correcting.
