Overview: Steps in solving a control problem

System thinking

  • What is the end goal of the control system, i.e. what is being measured

  • Understand the behaviour of the system
    • System dynamics
      • Forces acting on an actuator and on a system via the actuator. External forces acting on the system.

    • How is the parameter being measured affected by a commanded input

  • Sources of problems
    • Errors, timing delays, disturbances.

System identification

  • What parts of a system to model
  • What representation or model to use

Methods

White box method involves writing out equations of motion in the form of differential equations. Black box method involves conducting system tests in order to find unknown variables.
