Overview

Such a problem is encountered with a robot that has many DOFs that needs to achieve several tasks that are often in conflict under constraints.

References

The main paper: Generalising the task priority framework to inequality tasks.

This work is often referred to as a hierarchy of quadratic programs.

Notation swap

The solution to a single task can be written more abstractly as: $$\begin{align*} \min_x \quad & x^T A x + b^T x \\ s.t. \quad & Cx \leq x_{lim} \end{align*}$$ The $C$ matrix in the inequality allows the inequality constraints to be mapped to the cartesian space. E.g., $C$ could be a task Jacobian which allows incorporating constraints such as keeping the camera focused on a particular region of a workspace. It can then be mapped to the joint space and incorporated into different solvers.
