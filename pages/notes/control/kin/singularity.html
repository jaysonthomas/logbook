Cause Solution: Damped least squares

Singularities

Cause

This happens when the determinant of the jacobian is 0, i.e. the left pseudo inverse cannot be computed. The whole equation breaks down. $$ det(J) = 0 $$

Solution: Damped least squares

The damped LS solution reformulates the optimisation problem to include some relaxation. $$ \min_{\dot{q}} \quad \|v - J\dot{q}\|^2 $$ Expanding the equation: $$ \min_{\dot{q}} \quad \dot{q}^T \underbrace{J^T J}_{\text{Hessian} \atop \text{matrix}} \dot{q} - 2 \dot{q}^T J^T v $$ Adding a damping term to the above quadratic form by adding a small $\epsilon$ and the identity matrix to the Hessian matrix, the Hessian matrix ends up always having an inverse. The solution, hence computed, will always work even at a singularity at the cost of more inaccuracies in the output that the diff IK algorithm gives. $$ \min_{\dot{q}} \quad \dot{q}^T (J^T J + \epsilon I) \dot{q} - 2 \dot{q}^T J^T v $$ There is a lot of work on how to choose the $\epsilon$ or even how to dynamically change it depending on the distance from a singularity.
