Cart-pole dynamics: Forces acting on the cart

Overview

External forces on the cart:
  • The external horizontal force $f$
  • A force transmitted by the pendulum rod at point A resulting from the inertial resistance of the pendulum mass at point G
  • The cart's own inertial resistance, i.e. the cart's resistance to acceleration.
  • Gravity acting on the pendulum mass
Given that the cart does not move vertically, the analysis is constrained here to the horizontal forces acting on the cart, i.e. the first 3 forces. Summation of these forces will equate to zero as per d'Alembert's principle (since they are all in the $\hat{i}$ direction, the unit vector will not be explicitly specified).

Equation of motion

$$\begin{align*} f - m^{\scriptsize{c}}\, \ddot{x} + F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i &= 0 \nonumber\\ m^{\scriptsize{c}}\, \ddot{x} - m^{\scriptsize{p}} \biggl(l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) - \ddot{x} \biggr) &= f \\ \end{align*}$$

$\ddot{x}$

$$ \ddot{x} = \frac{f + m^{\scriptsize{p}}l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta)} {m^{\scriptsize{p}} + m^{\scriptsize{c}}} \tag{3} \label{eq:3} $$

$\ddot{\theta}$

$$ \ddot{\theta} = \frac{(m^{\scriptsize{c}} + m^{\scriptsize{p}}) \ddot{x} - m^{\scriptsize{p}} l \dot{\theta}^2 \cos\theta - f} {m^{\scriptsize{p}} l \sin\theta} \tag{4} \label{eq:4} $$ This equation is avoided to obtain $\ddot{\theta}$ as it would return an undefined result when $sin\theta$ in the denominator is $0$ for $\theta=0$.
