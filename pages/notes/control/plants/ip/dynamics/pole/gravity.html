Pole dynamics: Moment due to gravity

Overview

The torque at A resulting from the downward pull of gravity on the pendulum's mass at G is given by: $$ \bigl|M^{\scriptsize{A}}_g\bigr| = -m^{\scriptsize{G}} g \,sin\theta \cdot l $$ The moment is in the $\hat{k}$ direction.

The moment is zero when the pendulum is fully vertical, and negative when the pendulum is at $+90^0$, i.e. gravity accelerates the pendulum with a negative torque thereby rotating it in the clockwise direction.

Any force along $\hat{d}$ does not convert to a torque about $\hat{k}$. The dot product of the pole's weight and $\hat{d}$, the unit vector in the direction of the pole, gives the component of the pole's weight in the direction of the pole, $m^{\scriptsize{G}} g\,cos\theta$.
