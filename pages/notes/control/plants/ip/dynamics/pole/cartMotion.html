Pole dynamics: Inertial moment due to the cart's motion

Overview

Inertial force resulting from the inertial resistance of the pendulum's point mass at G as the cart moves left or right

Force

The pendulum's mass at point G resists the cart's acceleration, resulting in an inertial force with a direction opposite to the direction of acceleration. $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{i} &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}}\, \wrt{W}{0.1}{\ddot{x}}{G} \,\hat{i} \\ &= m_{\scriptsize{p}} \biggl(l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) - \ddot{x} \biggr) \,\hat{i} \\ (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{j} &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}}\, \wrt{W}{0.1}{\ddot{y}}{G} \,\hat{j} \\ &= m_{\scriptsize{p}} l(\ddot\theta \, cos\theta - \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta) \, \hat{j} \\ \end{align*}$$

Moment due to the force

The cross product of:
  • The radial vector AG, $l\hat{d}$
  • The inertial force at G, $F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i$
results in a vector that points along the axis of rotation, whose magnitude and sign is the same as that of a torque vector at pivot point A due to force $F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i$. Since the axis of rotation is fixed and is parallel with the z-axis, the torque vector's magnitude and sign is given by the z-axis component alone.
$$\begin{align*} (M^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{k} &= l\hat{d} \times F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i \\ &= (l\hat{d})\hat{i} \cdot (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{j} - (l\hat{d})\hat{j} \cdot (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{i} \\ &= \biggl[l\,sin\theta \cdot m_{\scriptsize{p}} l(\ddot\theta \, cos\theta - \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta) \biggr] - \biggl[l\,cos\theta \cdot m_{\scriptsize{p}} \biggl(l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) - \ddot{x}\biggr)\biggr] \\ &= m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta \,sin\theta cos\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \dot\theta^2 \, sin^2\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta \,sin\theta cos\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \dot\theta^2 \,cos^2\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta \\ &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \dot\theta^2 + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta \\ &= m_{\scriptsize{p}} l (\ddot{x} \,cos\theta - l \dot\theta^2) \\ \end{align*}$$
