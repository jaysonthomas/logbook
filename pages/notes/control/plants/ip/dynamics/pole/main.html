Dynamics: Pole

Topics

A pendulum on a cart experiences the following 3 torques; all of which must be in equilibrium as per d'Alembert's principle. These torques contribute to 1 equation of motion for the cart and pole model:
  • Moment due to gravity
  • Balance of forces
  • Balance of moments
  • Inertial moments due to resistance to the pole's:
    • Linear acceleration
    • Rotational acceleration

Equation of motion

Summing the three moments and equating to zero as per d'Alembert's principle, gives: $$\begin{align*} (M_g + M^{\scriptsize{G}}_i + M^{\scriptsize{A}}_i)\,\hat{k} &= 0 \\ -m_{\scriptsize{p}}g l\,sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta - J \ddot\theta &= 0 \end{align*}$$

$\ddot{x}$

$$ \ddot{x} = \frac{-J \ddot\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}}g l\,sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta} {m_{\scriptsize{p}}l\,cos\theta} \tag{1} \label{eq:1} $$ This equation is avoided to obtain $\ddot{x}$ as it would return an undefined result when $cos\theta$ in the denominator is $0$ for $\theta=90^0$.

$\ddot{\theta}$

$$ \ddot\theta = \frac{-m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}}g l\,sin\theta } {J + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2} \tag{2} \label{eq:2} $$
