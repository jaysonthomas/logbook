Cart-pole dynamics: Forces acting on the pole

Overview

A pendulum on a cart experiences the following 3 torques; all of which must be in equilibrium as per d'Alembert's principle. These torques contribute to 1 equation of motion for the cart and pole model:
  • Moment due to gravity
  • Inertial moment due to the cart's motion
  • A rotational inertial force known as the moment of inertia

Moment of inertia

The moment of inertia is initially written as some multiple $J$ of $\ddot\theta$, to be substituted later. $$ (M^{\scriptsize{A}}_i) \,\hat{k} = J \ddot\theta $$

Equation of motion

Summing the three moments and equating to zero as per d'Alembert's principle, gives: $$\begin{align*} (M_g + M^{\scriptsize{G}}_i + M^{\scriptsize{A}}_i)\,\hat{k} &= 0 \\ -m_{\scriptsize{p}}g l\,sin\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l (\ddot{x} \,cos\theta - l \dot\theta^2) + J \ddot\theta &= 0 \end{align*}$$

$\ddot{x}$

$$ \ddot{x} = \frac{-J \ddot\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}}g l\,sin\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}}l^2 \dot\theta^2} {m_{\scriptsize{p}}l\,cos\theta} \tag{1} \label{eq:1} $$ This equation is avoided to obtain $\ddot{x}$ as it would return an undefined result when $cos\theta$ in the denominator is $0$ for $\theta=90^0$.

$\ddot{\theta}$

$$\begin{align*} &\ddot\theta = \frac{m_{\scriptsize{p}}l \bigl[l \dot\theta^2 - \ddot{x} \,cos\theta + g sin\theta\bigr]}{J} \tag{2} \label{eq:2} \end{align*}$$
