Pole's resistance to its linear acceleration

Overview

Inertial force, resulting from the inertial resistance of a pendulum's point mass at G to its linear acceleration, induces an inertial moment about A.

Force

The pole's mass at point G resists its linear acceleration, resulting in an inertial force with a direction opposite to the direction of its linear acceleration. $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{i} &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}}\, \wrt{W}{0.1}{\ddot{x}}{G} \,\hat{i} \\ &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}} \biggl(l(\ddot\theta \, cos\theta - \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta) + \ddot{x} \biggr) \,\hat{i} \\ &= (-m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot\theta \, cos\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} \ddot{x}) \,\hat{i} \\ (F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{j} &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}}\, \wrt{W}{0.1}{\ddot{y}}{G} \,\hat{j} \\ &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}} l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) \, \hat{j} \\ &= (-m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot\theta \, sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l\dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) \, \hat{j} \\ \end{align*}$$

Moment due to the force

The cross product of:
  • The radial vector AG, $l\hat{d}$
  • The inertial force at G, $F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i$
results in a vector that points along the axis of rotation $\hat{k}$, whose magnitude and sign is the same as that of a torque vector at pivot point A due to force $F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i$.
$$\begin{align*} \bigl|M^{\scriptsize{G}}_i\bigr| &= l\hat{d} \times F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i \\ &= |(l\hat{d})\hat{i}| \cdot |(F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{j}| - |(l\hat{d})\hat{j}| \cdot |(F^{\scriptsize{G}}_i)\,\hat{i}| \\ &= \biggl[l\,sin\theta (-m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot\theta \, sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l\dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) \biggr] - \biggl[-l\,cos\theta (-m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot\theta \, cos\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} \ddot{x})\biggr] \\ &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta \,sin^2\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta cos\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta \,cos^2\theta + m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \dot\theta^2 \,cos\theta sin\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta \\ &= -m_{\scriptsize{p}} l^2 \ddot\theta - m_{\scriptsize{p}} l \ddot{x} \,cos\theta \end{align*}$$
