Pole: Linear velocity due to rotation

Overview

If a pole rotates about $\hat{k}$ with an angular velocity: $$ \vec{\omega} = \dot\theta \hat{k} $$ Linear velocity, purely due to rotation, of a point located at a distance $\hat{r}$ from the axis of rotation is given as: $$ \vec{v} = \vec{\omega} \times \vec{r} $$ The vector product gives the magnitude and direction of $\vec{v}$.

Direction

The direction of $\vec{v}$ is always perpendicular to both the angular velocity vector and the position vector from the axis of rotation. The direction may not be along a known vector.

To find the projection of the resultant linear velocity vector on a known basis vector, the dot product is computed.

Derivation

Considering:
  • Unit vector cross product identities
  • Unit vector representations in basis vectors
$$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \newcommand{\avg}{\wrt{A}{-0.1}{v}{G}} \newcommand{\arg}{\wrt{A}{-0.5}{r}{G}} \newcommand{\wwg}{\wrt{A}{0}{\omega}{G}} \newcommand{\wwgTwo}{\wrt{W}{-0.5}{\omega}{G}} \wwgTwo \times \arg &= \dot\theta\,\hat{k} \times l\,\hat{d} \\ &= \dot\theta\,\hat{k} \times \bigl(l\,sin\theta \, \hat{i} -l\,cos\theta \, \hat{j}\bigr) \\ &= l\dot\theta \biggl[sin\theta \, (\hat{k} \times \hat{i}) -cos\theta \, (\hat{k} \times \hat{j})\biggr] \\ &= l\dot\theta \bigl(sin\theta \, \hat{j} +cos\theta \, \hat{i}\bigr) \\ &= l\dot\theta\,\hat{q} \end{align*}$$
