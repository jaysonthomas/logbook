Position of a point w.r.t. its axis of rotation

Overview

The position of G relative to A in the ${W}$ coordinate frame, for a given pendulum angle $\theta$ is given by: $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \wrt{A}{0.1}{x}{G} &= l\,sin\theta \, \hat{i} \\ \wrt{A}{0.1}{y}{G} &= -l\,cos\theta \, \hat{j} \end{align*}$$

The position vector between the 2 points is given as: $$\begin{align*} ^{\scriptsize{A}}r^{\scriptsize{G}} &= l\,\hat{d} \\ &= l\,sin\theta \, \hat{i} -l\,cos\theta \, \hat{j} \end{align*}$$
