Linear velocity of a pole attached to a cart

Overview

Considering:
  • Cart's linear velocity
  • A point's linear velocity w.r.t. its axis of rotation
  • Linear velocity purely due to rotation
The linear velocity of the pole is given as: $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \wrt{W}{-0.3}{v}{G} &= \wrt{W}{-0.9}{v}{A} + \wrt{A}{-0.4}{v}{G} \\ &= \dot{x}\, \hat{i} + l\dot\theta\,\hat{q} \\ &= \dot{x}\, \hat{i} + l\dot\theta \bigl(sin\theta \, \hat{j} +cos\theta \, \hat{i}\bigr) \\ \end{align*}$$ This same equation can be arrived at by taking the derivative of the position of G w.r.t. A to find $\wrt{A}{0}{v}{G}$: $$\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{-0.1}{x}{G} &= l \, \dot\theta \, cos\theta \, \hat{i} \\ \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{-0.1}{y}{G} &= l \, \dot\theta \, sin\theta \, \hat{j} \\ \end{align*}$$
