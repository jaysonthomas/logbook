Linear velocity of a point w.r.t. its axis of rotation

Overview

$$ \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \newcommand{\avg}{\wrt{A}{-0.1}{v}{G}} \newcommand{\arg}{\wrt{A}{-0.5}{r}{G}} \newcommand{\wwg}{\wrt{A}{0}{\omega}{G}} \newcommand{\wwgTwo}{\wrt{W}{-0.5}{\omega}{G}} \avg = \,\avg\Bigr|_{\wwg=0} + \bigl(\,\wwgTwo \times \arg\,\bigr) $$ $\avg\Bigr|_{\wwg=0}$ is the velocity of G as seen from A when G is not rotating. If G does not have any linear motions, the term is $0$.

$^{\scriptsize{A}}r^{\scriptsize{G}}$ is the position vector between the 2 points.
