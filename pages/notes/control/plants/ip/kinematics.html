Pole: Kinematics

FBD

Relative to A

Position

The position of G relative to A in the ${W}$ coordinate frame, for a given pendulum angle $\theta$ is given by: $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \wrt{A}{0.1}{x}{G} &= l\,cos\theta \, \hat{i} \tag{1} \label{eq:1}\\ \wrt{A}{0.1}{y}{G} &= -l\,sin\theta \, \hat{j} \tag{2} \label{eq:2}\\ \end{align*}$$

Linear velocity

Taking the derivative of \ref{eq:1} and \ref{eq:2} w.r.t. time gives the velocity of G relative to A in the X,Y axes, for a given pendulum angle $\theta$ and angular velocity $\dot\theta$. $$\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{0.1}{x}{G} &= -l \, \dot\theta \, sin\theta \, \hat{i} \tag{3} \label{eq:3} \\ \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{0.1}{y}{G} &= -l \, \dot\theta \, cos\theta \, \hat{j} \tag{4} \label{eq:4} \\ \end{align*}$$

Linear acceleration

Taking the derivative of \ref{eq:3} and \ref{eq:4} w.r.t. time gives the XY acceleration of G relative to A in the X,Y axes, for a given pendulum angle $\theta$, angular velocity $\dot\theta$ and angular acceleration $\ddot\theta$. $$\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{0.1}{\dot{x}}{G} &= (-l \, \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta -l \, \ddot\theta \, sin\theta) \, \hat{i} \\ &= -l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) \, \hat{i} \tag{5} \label{eq:5} \\ \frac{d}{dt}\,\wrt{A}{0.1}{\dot{y}}{G} &= (l \, \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta -l \, \ddot\theta \, cos\theta) \, \hat{j} \\ &= -l(\ddot\theta \, cos\theta - \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta) \, \hat{j} \tag{6} \label{eq:6} \\ \end{align*}$$

Relative to W

The absolute acceleration of G (i.e. acceleration relative to W) is obtained by adding the x-axis acceleration of A to \ref{eq:5}. The cart does not move vertically, therefore the absolute y-axis acceleration is remains the same as $\wrt{A}{0.1}{\ddot{y}}{G}$ $$\begin{align*} \wrt{W}{0.1}{\ddot{x}}{G} &= \wrt{A}{0.4}{\ddot{x}}{G} + \ddot{x} \, \hat{i} \\ &= \biggl(-l(\ddot\theta \, sin\theta + \dot\theta^2 \, cos\theta) + \ddot{x} \biggr) \, \hat{i} \tag{7} \label{eq:7} \\ \wrt{W}{0.2}{\ddot{y}}{G} &= \wrt{A}{0.5}{\ddot{y}}{G} + \ddot{y} \, \hat{j} \\ &= \biggl(-l(\ddot\theta \, cos\theta - \dot\theta^2 \, sin\theta) + 0 \biggr) \, \hat{j} \tag{8} \label{eq:8} \\ \end{align*}$$
