Cartpole dynamics: Description of forces/moments

Overview

$$\begin{array}{cclc} N &:& \text{Normal force of the track pushing up on the cart} \\ f_x &:& \text{Horizontal force of the motor} \\ W_c &:& \text{Weight of the cart} \\ W_p &:& \text{Weight of the pole} \\ F_1, F_2 &:& \text{2 components of the tension (also called constraint force or conduct force)} \\ &&\text{experienced by the rod} \\ N_1 &:& \text{Normal force experienced by the wheels} \\ \end{array}$$

Cart

A motor applies a horizontal force $f_x$. For any positive motion, the spring force resists and pulls back. For any positive velocity, the damper pulls back.

Friction and the reaction forces at the wheels are ignored.

Because of Newton's 3rd law, there are 2 forces acting on the cart at A that are equal and opposite to those acting on the pole at that point.

Pole

There are 2 possible forces $F_1, F_2$ acting on the pole at the point of rotation (attachment to the cart) at A. They are not drawn arbitrarily; their directions have been chosen. But, their directions are not known.

Gravity acting on the pole

The entire mass of the pole is collapsed to a point at the end of it to keep the maths simple.
The component of the pole's weight in the direction of the pole is $m^{\scriptsize{G}} g\,cos\theta$.
