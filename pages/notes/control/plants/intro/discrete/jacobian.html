Jacobian

Setup

Consider the linearised discrete time dynamics with the origin shifted to the equilibrium point: $$ x_{k+1} \approx A_d \, x_k $$
$$\begin{array}{cclc} A_d &:& \text{Jacobian matrix. Constant.} \\ x_{k+1} &:& \text{Dependent variable} \\ x_k &:& \text{Independent variable for the specific step} \\ \end{array}$$

Differentiating a single step

Derivative of $x_{k+1}$ with respect to $x_k$: $$\begin{align*} \frac{\partial x_{k+1}}{\partial x_k} &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x_k} [A_d x_k] \\ &= A_d \end{align*}$$ For any step in the linearised system, the sensitivity of the next state to the current state is simply the Jacobian matrix $A_d$.

Unrolling the chain rule

Applying the chain rule to find the derivative of the $N$-th step w.r.t. the initial state $x_0$ (assumed to be at the equilibrium point): $$\begin{align*} \frac{\partial x_N}{\partial x_0} &= \frac{\partial x_N}{\partial x_{N-1}} \cdot \frac{\partial x_{N-1}}{\partial x_{N-2}} \cdot \dots \cdot \frac{\partial x_1}{\partial x_0} \\ &= A_d \cdot A_d \cdot \dots \cdot A_d \\ &= A_d^N \end{align*}$$
