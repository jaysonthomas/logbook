Stability of discrete-time systems

Stability criteria

Assuming the origin of the coordinate system is the equilibrium point, after $k$ time steps $$ \lim_{k \to \infty} A_d^k x_0 = 0 \quad \forall x_0 $$ As $k$ goes to infinity, for stability, $A_d^k x_0$ must go to 0 asymptotically, i.e. the $k+1^\text{th}$ state must be at the equilibrium point, at the origin.

If the above must be true for any $x_0$, then it must be true for $A_d$.

$$ \lim_{k \to \infty} A_d^k = 0 $$ This means the eigenvalues of the $A_d$ matrix needs to be within the unit circle (the norm must be less than 1). $$ |\text{eigvals}(A_d)| < 1 $$ This stability criteria is valid for all linear systems. For a non-linear system that has been linearised, this is valid for some local neighbourhood near an equilibrium point.
