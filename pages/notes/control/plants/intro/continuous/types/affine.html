Control affine systems

It is a non-linear system, that is non-linear in the state, but linear-affine in the control input. $$ \dot{x} = f_0(x) + B(x)\, u $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} f_0(x) &:& \text{Drift} \\ B(x) &:& \text{Input jacobian matrix} \end{array}$$
$f_0$ and $B$ are non-linear functions of $x$. If $x$ is fixed, then thinking of the equation as a function in $u$, $f_0$ and $B$ become constants and the equation becomes affine in the control input.

$f_0$ and $B$ are generally non-zero. If $B$ is full rank (i.e. a system is fully actuated), the equation can be inverted and can be solved for $u$ for any arbitrary $\dot{x}$. Feedback linearisation is commonly used in this case to control classical manipulator arms.
