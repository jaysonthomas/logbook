Manipulator

Overview

All mechanical systems (multi-body, rigid body robot systems) can be written in this form. It is another way of writing the Euler-Lagrange equation. $$ M(q) \, \dot{v} + C(q, v) = B(q) \, u + F $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} M(q) &:& \text{Mass matrix that is a function of pose. Also called the generalised inertia tensor.} \\ \dot{v} &:& \text{Acceleration} \\ C(q, v) &:& \text{Dynamic bias or Coriolis + gravity} \\ B(q) &:& \text{Input jacobian} \\ F &:& \text{External forces on the system apart from control, e.g. contact forces} \\ \end{array}$$

Velocity kinematics

Since $\dot{q}$ is not always the same as $\omega$ or $v$ (e.g. with the use of quaternions for attitude representation, $\omega$ is a $3 \times 1$ vector, whereas $q$ and hence $\dot{q}$ is a $4 \times 1$ vector. Similarly with a rotation matrix.), the following velocity kinematics is used: $$ \dot{q} = G(q) \, v $$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} G(q) &:& \text{Kinematic jacobian that is a function of }q \end{array}$$
If $v$ or $\omega$ is the same as $\dot{q}$, then $G = 1$.

First order ODE form

The state space form would then be: $$\begin{align*} \dot{x} &= f(x,u) \\ \begin{bmatrix} \dot\theta \\ \ddot{\theta} \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} G(q)\, v \\ M^{-1}q \, \left(B(q)\, u + F - C \right) \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}$$ Simple pendulum example.

Mass matrix

In the Euler-Lagrange equation, $M$ is the mass matrix written in joint coordinates. Since it's in the quadratic form in the kinetic energy equation, it has to be symmetric.

From basic physics arguments, since kinetic energy cannot be negative, $M$ has to be symmetric positive definite, i.e., has to be invertible.

It is possible to choose a bad set of coordinates (e.g. introduce redundant coordinates that don't do anything) that then make $M$ ill-conditioned or have $0$ eigen values and be singular or cause $M$ to have $0$s.
