Navier Stokes equation

Overview

For an incompressible fluid like water, the equations describe a balance of forces. Imagine a cube of water moving through a pipe:
  • Mass Conservation (Continuity): Water isn't "squishy." If you push 1 liter into one end of a pipe, 1 liter must come out the other. You can't create or destroy water volume.

  • Momentum Conservation: The change in velocity of that water cube is determined by the sum of forces acting on it:

Pressure: Water moves from high-pressure zones to low-pressure zones (like squeezing a tube of toothpaste). Viscosity (Friction): Think of this as the "thickness" or internal friction. It smooths out velocity differences between layers of water. External Forces: Gravity pulling it down or a pump pushing it forward.
