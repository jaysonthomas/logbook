Viscous (Friction) Force

Overview

$$\frac{1}{Re}\nabla^2 u$$ Fluids have internal friction (viscosity) that resists sliding. The $\nabla^2 u$ term represents how much the local velocity "differs" from its surroundings. If a parcel of fluid is moving much faster than its neighbors, friction drags it back. The strength of this effect is scaled by $\frac{1}{Re}$.
