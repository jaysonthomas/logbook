Aim

At each time step, the velocity field $u$ at the current time step $k$ is known. The aim is to find $u_{k+1}$.

This is achieved by:

  • Solving the Navier-Stokes momentum equation numerically.
  • Integrating with a Crank-Nicolson scheme (implicit midpoint) which steps forward in time.

$$\begin{equation} \frac{u_{k+1}-u_{k}}{\Delta t}+\frac{1}{2}\left[N\left(u_{k+1}\right)+N\left(u_{k}\right)\right]=\frac{1}{2 R e}\left[L u_{k+1}+L u_{k}+2 b c_{L}\right]-G p_{k+1} \end{equation}$$ Rearranging to put all $k+1$ terms on the left: $$\begin{equation} \underbrace{\left(\frac{1}{\Delta t} I-\frac{1}{2 R e} L\right)}_{A} u_{k+1}+\frac{1}{2} N\left(u_{k+1}\right)+G p_{k+1}=\underbrace{\left(\frac{1}{\Delta t} I+\frac{1}{2 R e} L\right) u_{k}-\frac{1}{2} N\left(u_{k}\right)+\frac{1}{R e} b c_{L}}_{r+b c_{1}} \end{equation}$$
