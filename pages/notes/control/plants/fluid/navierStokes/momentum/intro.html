Introduction

Overview

It is a 2nd order equation in time. It helps in understanding fluid flow - the change in velocity of a fluid-cube is determined by the sum of forces acting on it:
  • Pressure: Water moves from high-pressure zones to low-pressure zones (like squeezing a tube of toothpaste).

  • Viscosity (Friction) is the internal friction. It smooths out velocity differences between layers of water.

  • External Forces: Gravity pulling it down or a pump pushing it forward.

Math

$$ \underbrace{\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + (u \cdot \nabla)u}_{ma} = \underbrace{-\nabla p + \frac{1}{Re}\nabla^2 u + a_{ext}}_{F} $$ This equation tracks the total acceleration of a specific 'parcel' of fluid and balances it against all the forces acting on it.
