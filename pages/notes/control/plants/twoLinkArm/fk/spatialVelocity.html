Spatial Velocity

Overview

Spatial algebra for velocities called spatial velocity $\mathbb{R}^{6 \times 1}$ is similar to spatial algebra for positions and orientations. It has a translation and an orientation component: $$\begin{bmatrix} \omega \\ v \end{bmatrix}$$ $\omega$ is the angular velocities $\mathbb{R}^{3 \times 1}$
$v$ is the translational velocities $\mathbb{R}^{3 \times 1}$

There is no standard/canonical choice of representation for a pose. Based on a chosen representation, an orientation (which is part of a pose), can have various problems hence the need for constantly moving between representations. However there is a canonical choice for angular velocity, consisting of 3 numbers representing the rotation rates around the major axis.
