Dynamics of a single rigid body

Center of mass

[Transport theorem]
A rigid body is a collection of point masses that are rigidly attached to each other. Center of mass is defined to be the unique point at the centroid of the mass distribution with a frame {b} attached to it. The sum of mass-weighted vectors from the centre of mass to all the point masses will be $0$: $$ \sum_i {m_i p_i} = 0 $$

Twist

[Ref]

Definition

Twist is a 6-vector consisting of:
  • 3d angular velocity vector
  • 3d linear velocity vector
    • It is the linear velocity of a point at the origin of that frame.
Both are written in coordinates of the same frame.

Types

Both the body twist and the spatial twist represent the same motion, just in different coordinate frames. The body twist is not affected by the choice of the space frame, and the spatial twist is not affected by the choice of the body frame. $$ V_b = \begin{bmatrix} \omega_b \\ v_b \end{bmatrix} $$
