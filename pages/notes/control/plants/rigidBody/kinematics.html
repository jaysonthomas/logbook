Rigid Body: Kinematics

Overview

By integrating acceleration once w.r.t. time to find velocity and integrating the velocity once w.r.t. time and incorporating the intial conditions at each step, the final position can be found.

Using acceleration

Definition

Assuming acceleration is constant: $$ a = \frac{dv(t)}{dt} $$

Integrating w.r.t. time

Integrating both sides with respect to time: $$\begin{align} \int a dt &= \int \frac{dv(t)}{dt} dt \nonumber \\ at + C &= v(t) \label{eq:integrateAccel} \end{align}$$
$$\begin{array}{ccl} v(t) &:& \text{Velocity is the rate of change of position w.r.t. time.}\\ C &:& \text{Integration constant} \end{array}$$

Finding the integration constant

Substituting the initial condition defined by $t=0$ and $v(0) = v_0$, in \ref{eq:integrateAccel}: $$\begin{align*} a \cdot 0 + C &= v(0) \\ C &= v_0 \end{align*}$$ \ref{eq:integrateAccel} becomes: $$ v(t) = v_0 + at $$

Using velocity

Definition

$$\begin{align*} v(t) &= \frac{dy(t)}{dt} \\ &= v_0 + at \end{align*}$$

Integrating w.r.t. time

$$\begin{align} \int (v_0 + at) dt &= \frac{dy(t)}{dt} dt \nonumber \\ v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 + C &= y(t) \label{eq:integrateVel} \end{align}$$

Finding the integration constant

Substituting the initial condition defined by $t=0$ and $y(0) = p_0$, in \ref{eq:integrateVel}: $$\begin{align*} v_0 \cdot 0 + \frac{1}{2}a \cdot 0^2 + C &= p_0 \\ C &= p_0 \end{align*}$$ \ref{eq:integrateVel} becomes: $$ y(t) = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 + p_0 $$
