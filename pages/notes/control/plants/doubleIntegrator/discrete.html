Discrete time

State update

The next state is computed from the current state and control input. $$\begin{align*} x_{k+1} &= Ax_k + Bu_k \\ &= \begin{bmatrix}1 & h \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}q \\ \dot{q} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2}h^2 \\ h \end{bmatrix} u_k \\ \end{align*}$$ $h$ - time step

This is a simple system that has a simple closed form solution.

State space model

Dynamics

$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & h \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ This corresponds to: $$\begin{align*} q_{k+1} &= q_k + h\dot q_k \\ \dot q_{k+1} &= \dot q_k \\ \end{align*}$$ Without control, velocity stays constant.

Control input

$$ B = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2}h^2 \\ h \end{bmatrix} $$ This says acceleration $u$ affects:
  • position through $\frac{1}{2}h^2$
  • velocity through $hu$

Simulation method

This solution can be obtained from RK4. In this system, the highest order in the $A$ and $B$ matrices is second order. Hence, in this instance, RK4 would give an exact solution since it's accurate up to the fourth order in the Taylor expansion approximation.
