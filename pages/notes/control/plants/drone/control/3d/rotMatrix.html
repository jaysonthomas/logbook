Rotation matrix

Overview

The following rotation matrix $R$ is used to transform between body-frame and world-frame accelerations: $$ R = R_z(\psi) \cdot R_y(\theta) \cdot R_x(\phi) $$ The $x$, $y$, and $z$ rotations are: $$\begin{alignedat}{1} R_x(\phi) &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos\phi & -\sin\phi \\ 0 & \sin\phi & \cos\phi \\ \end{bmatrix} \\ R_y(\theta) &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & 0 & \sin\theta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin\theta & 0 & \cos\theta \\ \end{bmatrix} \\ R_z(\psi) &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\psi & -\sin\psi & 0 \\ \sin\psi & \cos\psi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{alignedat}$$
