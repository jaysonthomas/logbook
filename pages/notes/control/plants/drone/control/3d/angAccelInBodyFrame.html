Angular accelerations in body frame

Overview

Using Euler's equation in a rotating frame, the angular acceleration in body frame can be calculated as: $$ I \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \dot{p} \\ \dot{q} \\ \dot{r} \end{bmatrix}}_{\dot\omega} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \tau_x \\ \tau_y \\ \tau_z \end{bmatrix}}_{M} - \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} p \\ q \\ r \end{bmatrix}}_{\omega} \times I \begin{bmatrix} p \\ q \\ r \end{bmatrix} $$ $$\begin{align*} I_x \dot{p} &= \tau_x - (I_z-I_y) r q \\ I_y \dot{q} &= \tau_y - (I_x-I_z) p r \\ I_z \dot{r} &= \tau_z - (I_y-I_x) q p \\ \end{align*}$$ The angular accelerations in the body frame can then be integrated to get the angular velocities $p,q,r$ in the body frame.
