Angular velocities in world frame

Overview

Instantaneous turn rates in the body frame can be converted to instantaneous changes in the drone's angles in the world frame by the following equation: $$ \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\phi} \\ \dot{\theta} \\ \dot{\psi} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & s(\phi) t(\theta) & c(\phi) t(\theta) \\ 0 & c(\phi) & -s(\phi) \\ 0 & s(\phi)sec(\theta) & c(\phi)sec(\theta) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} p \\ q \\ r \end{bmatrix} $$

$sec(\theta) = \frac{1}{cos(\theta)}$

Euler angles are used under the hood because they have a more intuitive interpretation than quaternions [Refer to equation 79 in James Diebel's paper]. If a different attitude representation is used, the above equation would change, but would still have the same function of converting from rotation rates in the body frame to turn rates in the world frame.
