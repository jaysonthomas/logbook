3D control: Steps

Overview

Generally, the inner control loops that operate on a faster timescale are tuned first. The sequence in order would be: Body-rate, Roll-pitch, Altitude, Lateral and finally Yaw. The Yaw controller is independent of the others and is left to the last.

Once the body-rate control can be relied on to hit its roll-pitch yaw rate commands, then the roll-pitch control can be initially tuned by providing enough thrust to roughly counteract gravity.

Overall summary

Complex controls problems can be broken down into a sequence of first and second order systems. Second order systems can be controlled with PD or PID controllers.

A second order system can sometimes be controlled with two cascaded P controllers, with each controlling a separate first order system. For a drone, the cascaded P modules have some physically meaningful parameters that can be tuned to adjust the behavior of the system.

In 3D, orientation is thought of in both the body frame and the world frame. Converting between the two introduces some mathematical complexity that a controller has to handle.

Way points generated by a 3D motion planner can be used as input to a 3D quadrotor controller. The afore mentioned steps form part of the full stack of a flying vehicle.
