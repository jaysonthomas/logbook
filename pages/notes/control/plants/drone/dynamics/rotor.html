Rotor dynamics

Forces

Each spinning rotor produces a thrust force upwards given by: $$\begin{align*} F_t &\propto \omega^2 \\ &= k_f \,\omega^2 \\ \end{align*}$$ The constant of proportionality, $k_f$ is the thrust coefficient. When the rotation speed of a rotor is doubled, thrust increases by a factor of 4.

Moments

The torque/moment produced by a spinning rotor is proportional to $\omega^2$: $$\begin{align*} M &\propto \omega^2 \\ &= k_m \,\omega^2 \end{align*}$$
$$\begin{array}{cclc} k_m &:& \text{Angular torque coefficient} & [Nm\frac{s^2}{rad^2}] \\ \omega &:& \text{Rotation rate of a rotor} & [\frac{rad}{s}] \end{array}$$
When $\omega = 2\pi$, the rotor does one rotation per second.

Lift constants

Constants $k_f, k_m$ depend on the:
  • Size and shape of the plates
  • Specific motors used
  • Density of the surrounding air they operate in
