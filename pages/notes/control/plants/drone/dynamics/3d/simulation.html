3D Drone dynamics: Simulation

Advance State

$$ X_{n+1} = X + (\dot{X} \cdot dt) $$ $$\begin{align*} X = \begin{bmatrix} x & y & z & \phi & \theta & \psi& \dot{x} & \dot{y} & \dot{z} & p & q & r \end{bmatrix}^T \end{align*}$$

Rotation

$p, q, r$ are the drone's angular rates about the x, y, and z axis respectively of the body frame. These are the rotational velocities that would be measured by a gyroscope mounted on the drone.

These are not the same as the derivatives of the drone's orientation represented in euler angles $\phi, \theta, \psi$, since the orientation is in world frame coordinates. They describe the rotations necessary to rotate the world frame into the body frame.

It is easier to work with body angular rates than with euler angle rates.

Translation

$x,y,z$ are positions of the drone relative to the world frame and $\dot{x},\dot{y},\dot{z}$ are translational velocities along these axes.
