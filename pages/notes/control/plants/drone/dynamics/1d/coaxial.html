Coaxial Helicopter dynamics

Overview

A co-axial helicopter is a body with two rotors on top of each other which produce upward thrusts by spinning in opposite directions. Let rotor 1 spin CW with $\omega_1$ and rotor 2 spin CCW with $\omega_2$.

Linear acceleration

$$\begin{align*} \ddot{z} &= \frac{F_{net}}{m} \\ &= \frac{1}{m}(mg - F_{thrust}) \\ &= \frac{1}{m}(mg - k_f(\omega_1^2 + \omega_2^2)) \\ &= g - \frac{k_f}{m}(\omega_1^2 + \omega_2^2) \end{align*}$$

Angular acceleration

$$\begin{align*} \ddot{\psi} &= \frac{M_{z}}{I_z} \\ &= \frac{k_m}{I_z}(\omega_1^2 - \omega_2^2) \end{align*}$$ The body of the vehicle experiences angular acceleration in the CCW direction if rotor 2 spins faster than rotor 1. The body and the propeller spin in opposite directions to each other. Both $\ddot{z}$ and $\ddot\psi$ are along the z axis. $\omega_2^2$ is given a negative sign because only the reaction torque is considered since that's the torque that produces motion.

Computing angular velocity

$$\begin{align*} \omega_1^2 + \omega_2^2 &= \frac{m}{k_f} \cdot (g - \ddot{z}) \\\\ \omega_1^2 - \omega_2^2 &= \frac{I_z}{k_m} \cdot \ddot{\psi} \\ |\omega_1| &= \sqrt{\frac{\frac{I_z}{k_m} \cdot \ddot{\psi} + \frac{m}{k_f} \cdot (g - \ddot{z})}{2}} \end{align*}$$ Since the second rotor rotates CCW, $\omega_2 = -|\omega_2|$.
