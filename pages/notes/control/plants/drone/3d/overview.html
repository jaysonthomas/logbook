3D Drone dynamics: Overview

Steps

  • How do the 4 propeller turn rates, turn into a net force and three moments?
    • $$ \text{Propeller Turn rates} \rightarrow F, M_x, M_y, M_z $$

  • How do the forces acting on a drone affect its translational motion?
    • $$ F \rightarrow \ddot{x}, \ddot{y}, \ddot{z} \rightarrow \dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z} \rightarrow x,y,z $$

  • How do the moments acting on a drone affect its rotational motion?
    • $$ M_x,M_y,M_z \xrightarrow [\scriptsize\text{equations}]{\scriptsize\text{Euler's}} \dot{p}, \dot{q}, \dot{r} \xrightarrow{\int} p,q,r \xrightarrow[\scriptsize\text{maths}]{\scriptsize\text{Magic}} \dot\phi, \dot\theta, \dot\psi \xrightarrow{\int} \phi, \theta, \psi $$ All of the $p,q,r$ notations are in the body frame which needs to be converted into the world frame representation.

Frames

A world frame is an inertial frame, i.e. fixed in the world, relative to the surroundings. A body frame is attached to the object under consideration.

Keeping track of the 2 frames is what makes 3D more difficult - a drone's change in attitude will need to be tracked in both these coordinate systems.

Ultimately the goal is to control the drone in the world frame. The following will need to be converted into the world frame:
  • Sensor measurements like those from the IMU, which measures turn rates in the body frame

  • Controls like the commanded moments have a much more intuitive interpretation when reasoned in the body frame
