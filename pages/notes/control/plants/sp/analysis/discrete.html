Discrete time stability analysis

With forward euler

$$\begin{align*} x_{k+1} &= \underbrace{x_k + h \, f(x_k, u_k)}_{f_d(x_k, u_k)} \\ A_d &= \frac{\partial{f_d}}{\partial{x_k}} \\ &= I + hA \\ &= I + h \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ \frac{-g \,cos\theta}{l} & 0 \end{bmatrix} \\ \text{eigvals}\left(A_d \big|_{\theta=0}\right) &\approx 1 \pm 0.313i \end{align*}$$ In the continuous case, the eigen values are purely imaginary. In the discrete case, they are exactly on the unit circle. The error in the imaginary part is due to the error in forward euler integration.
