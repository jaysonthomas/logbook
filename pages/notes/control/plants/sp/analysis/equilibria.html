Equilibria

Overview

Considering the dynamics of an uncontrolled simple pendulum, i.e. no applied torque: $$ \dot{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\theta} \\ \frac{-g \,sin\theta}{l} \end{bmatrix} $$ The points at which the state vector is 0 are when:
  • The pendulum is down, $\theta = 0$ or the pendulum is up, $\theta = \pi$ and
  • $\dot\theta = 0$

Example of moving the equilibrium point

To $\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}$. That is, at this angle, the aim would be to use control to force the pendulum to be at rest. $$ \dot{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \dot{\theta} \\ \frac{u -m_p g \,sin\theta \cdot l}{m_pl^2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} $$ Solving for the root $u$ in the 2nd row leads to $u = m_p g l$. This, simply compensates for (cancels out) gravity and is widely used in manipulators.
