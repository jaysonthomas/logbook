Simple pendulum: Force due to gravity

Overview

Force due to gravity acting on the pendulum is divided into 2:
  • Component acting along the string $(F^{\scriptsize{G}}_g)\,\hat{q}$
  • Component acting tangential and opposite to the direction of motion of the mass $(F^{\scriptsize{G}}_g)\,\hat{d}$ [Ref]
Moment due to gravity about A is: $$ \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} (M^{\scriptsize{A}}_g)\,\hat{k} = -m_{\scriptsize{p}} g \,sin\theta \cdot l $$

Comparison to a unit circle

A sine function is defined in such a way that $sin(60^0) = sin(120^0)$. It's various values are shown at $90^0$ points. This definition matches the physical behaviour of $\hat{d}$ component of the gravity vector and hence it is used.
