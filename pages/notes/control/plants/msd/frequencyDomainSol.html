Single mass: Frequency domain solution

Setup

Imagine a MSD system with no damping. When displaced, the mass would oscillate forever.

The equation of motion would be: $$\begin{equation} m\ddot{x}(t) + kx(t) = f(t) \label{eq:msd} \end{equation}$$

Using the sinusoidal waveform equation

The differential equation of motion can be solved by assuming the solution is in the form of a sinusoidal waveform and then solving for the 3 unknown coefficients, $A$, $\omega$ and $\phi$: $$ x(t) = Acos(\omega t + \phi) $$ Since there are three unknowns, three equations will be required to solve for them.

Using acceleration

$$\begin{align*} x(t) &= Acos(\omega t + \phi) \\ \dot{x}(t) &= -A\omega sin(\omega t + \phi) \\ \ddot{x}(t) &= -A\omega^2cos(\omega t + \phi) \end{align*}$$ Substituting in \ref{eq:msd}: $$ m \left[-A\omega^2cos(\omega t + \phi)\right] + k \left[Acos(\omega t + \phi)\right] = f(t) $$

Using known initial conditions

Initial position

Initially, there is no force applied to the system: $$\begin{align*} m \left[-A\omega^2cos(\omega t + \phi)\right] + k \left[Acos(\omega t + \phi)\right] &= 0 \\ m \omega^2 \left[\cancel{Acos(\omega t + \phi)}\right] &= k \left[\cancel{Acos(\omega t + \phi)}\right] \\ \omega &= \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \end{align*}$$ Hence, the initial position of the mass is at rest, resulting in: $$\begin{align*} x(0) &= 0 \\ Acos(\omega t + \phi) &= 0 \\ \phi &= \frac{\pi}{2} \end{align*}$$

Application of an impulse force

Taking the integral of the acceleration, an impulse force generates an instantaneous velocity as follows (integral of an impulse force is $1$): $$\begin{align*} v(t) &= \int^\infty_{-\infty} \frac{\delta(t)}{m}dt \\ &= \frac{1}{m} \scriptsize\text{ m\s} \end{align*}$$ A force applied in the negative direction to the mass would result in an instantaneous initial velocity: $$\begin{align*} \dot{x}(0) &= -\frac{1}{m} \\ -A\omega sin(\omega t + \phi) &= -\frac{1}{m} \\ A &= \frac{1}{\omega \cdot m \cdot sin(\phi)} \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{k \cdot m}} \end{align*}$$

Final solution

The motion of the block is described in time domain by a simple cosine wave: $$ x(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{km}}cos(\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}t + \frac{\pi}{2}) $$
