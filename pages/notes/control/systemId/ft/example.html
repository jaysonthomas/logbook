Fourier Transform: Example

Setup

Consider 2 functions in the time domain:
  • A simple exponential decay function $e^{-t}$
  • A step function $u(t)$
The result of multiplying the 2 signals is an exponential signal that is:
  • $0$ for $t < 0$
  • $1$ at $t = 0$
  • Decaying exponentially for $t > 0$

The fourier transform solution

Summary

A 1D time domain representation (signal value vs time) is converted into a 2D representation in the frequency domain (phase and magnitude vs frequency). The steps involved are:
  • The signal is first converted into its rectangular coordinate representation with real and imaginary components
  • It is then converted into polar coordinate representation with magnitude and phase components

Rectangular coordinate representation

Using a fourier transform table, the fourier transform of the above function $f(t)$: $$ \mathcal{F}(u(t)e^{-t}) = \frac{1}{1+j\omega} $$ This is a complex function, made up of two-dimensional numbers that have real and imaginary components. Separating out the 2 parts by multiplying by the complex conjugate to move imaginary variable $j$ out of the denominator: $$\begin{align*} &= \frac{1}{1+j\omega} \cdot \frac{1-j\omega}{1-j\omega} \\ &= \frac{1-j\omega}{1+\omega^2} \\ &= \underbrace{\frac{1}{1+\omega^2}}_{\scriptsize\text{Real component}} + \underbrace{j\frac{-\omega}{1+\omega^2}}_{\scriptsize\text{Imaginary component}} \\ \end{align*}$$

Polar coordinate representation

Phase as a function of frequency: $$\begin{align*} &= atan \left( \frac{\text{imag}}{\text{real}} \right) \\ &= atan \left( \frac{\frac{-\omega}{1+\omega^2}} {\frac{1}{1+\omega^2}} \right) \\ &= atan(-\omega) \end{align*}$$ Magnitude as a function of frequency: $$\begin{align*} &= \sqrt{\text{real}^2 + \text{imag}^2} \\ &= \sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{1+\omega^2}\right)^2 + \left( \frac{-\omega}{1+\omega^2}\right)^2} \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\omega^2}} \end{align*}$$
