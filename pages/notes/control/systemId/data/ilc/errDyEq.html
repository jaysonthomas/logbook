Error dynamics equation

Question

What guarantee is there that the error at $k+1$ is always less than the error at $k$? If so, then $U$ is being adjusted in a way that makes the system better at following the reference, since the error is being reduced.

Comparing errors at successive iterations

$$\begin{align*} E_{k+1} &= R - Y_{k+1} \\ &= R - G \, U_{k+1} \\ &= R - G \, (U_k + L\,E_k) \\ &= \underbrace{R - \overbrace{G \, U_k}^{Y_k}}_{E_k} - G \, L\,E_k \\ &= (I - GL) E_k \end{align*}$$ This is the Error dynamics equation which dictates how the error changes in an iteration.

For the error to be reduced, the following condition needs to be satisfied: $$ |I - GL| < 1 $$
