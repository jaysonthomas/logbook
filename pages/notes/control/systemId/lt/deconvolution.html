Deconvolution

Overview

Multiplication in the s domain is the same as convolution in the time domain. Therefore, division in the s domain is same as deconvolution in the time domain.

An example can be seen here when solving for $X(s)$.

Every time a simple multiplication or division is done with a transfer function, it is good to imagine the complicated maths that would have had to occur in the time domain to produce the same result. To go back to the time domain, an inverse Laplace transform on X(s) is taken (by referring to the Laplace conversion table).
