Clocks

Overview

A central clock, referred to as HCLK (High-speed Clock), drives the Arm Cortex-M4 core in an STM32F427IIT6 microcontroller. It has a maximum clock frequency (CPU speed) of 180 MHz.

Clocks for buses

A microcontroller also typically features several buses (shared communication pathway that transfers data and signals between components) for peripherals, which are derived from this main clock and have their own maximum speed limits:
  • AHB Buses (Advanced High-performance Bus): These buses connect high-speed peripherals. Their clock speed is typically derived directly from the HCLK, often running at the full 180 MHz.

  • APB Buses (Advanced Peripheral Bus): These buses connect to lower-speed peripherals.
    • APB2 (High-speed): Runs at a higher frequency (e.g., up to $\frac{180}{2} = 90$ MHz).
    • APB1 (Low-speed): Runs at a lower frequency (e.g., up to $\frac{180}{4} = 45$ MHz).

The exact speeds of the APB buses are configured using prescalers.
