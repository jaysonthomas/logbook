Position

The simplest concept is just a point in some three-dimensional space.
  • $A$ is the frame or point of interest
  • $P^A$ - Position of $A$

  • $^BP^A$ - Position of $A$ relative to $B$
    • $P$ is a vector going from point $B$ to $A$ - Imagine an arrow drawn from $B$ to $A$. Positions have to be defined w.r.t. something always.

  • $\in\mathbb{R}^3$
    • 3D vectors (3x1) contain 3 real numbers.

  • $^BP^A_C$ - $C$ is the expressed in frame
    • Distance between 2 people is given by $P$. But if I turn my back, the vector between the 2 points is still the same, but now it will be expressed using different numbers w.r.t. me looking the other way.

Inverse flips the sign: $-^BP^A_C = ^AP^B_C$

$P^A$ is the same as ${^W}P^A_W$. In drake visualisations, an RGB axis at the origin defines the world axis. If the expressed-in frame is not specified, it should be assumed that: $$^GP^A \equiv {^G}P^A_G$$
