Variables

Process noise is determined experimentally based on a system's model and usually remains constant. $v, p$ represent uncertainties. $$ q = \begin{bmatrix} v^2 & 0 \\ 0 & p^2 \end{bmatrix} $$ Are there always 2 uncertainty parameters p, v that feed into the process noise?

Prediction

$$\begin{align*} H &= h' = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \\ K &= \frac{\bar\Sigma \, H^T}{(H \, \bar\Sigma \, H^T) + R} \\ h &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \bar\mu \\ \mu &= \bar\mu + K(z - h) \\ \Sigma &= \left(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} - K \, H \right) \bar\Sigma \end{align*}$$
