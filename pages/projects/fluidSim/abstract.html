Abstract

Novelty

A differentiable fluid-structure interaction (FSI) solver for robotics that offers
  • stable simulation
    • Aquarium achieves stable simulation with accurate flow physics by directly integrating over the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations using a fully implicit Crank-Nicolson scheme with a second-order finite-volume spatial discretisation.

  • accurately coupled fluid-robot physics in two dimensions and
    • The fluid and robot physics are coupled using the immersed-boundary method by formulating the no-slip condition as an equality constraint applied directly to the Navier-Stokes system. This choice of coupling allows the fluid-structure interaction to be posed and solved as a nonlinear optimization problem.

  • full differentiability with respect to fluid and robot states and parameters
    • This optimization-based formulation is then exploited using the implicit-function theorem to compute derivatives. Derivatives can then be passed to downstream gradient-based optimization or learning algorithms.

Examples

Aquarium's ability to accurately simulate coupled fluid- robot physics with the following 2D examples:
  • cylinder in free stream
  • a soft robotic fish tail with hardware validation
  • Aquarium's ability to provide analytical gradients by performing gradient-based shape-and-gait optimization of an oscillating diamond foil to maximize its generated thrust.
