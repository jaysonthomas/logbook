Overview

The sensor used by the quadrotor estimates the altitude with a weighted average defined as: $$ \hat{x}_t = \alpha \hat{x}_{t-1} + (1-\alpha)y_t $$
$$\begin{array}{cclc} x_t &:& \text{True state of the drone at time t} \\ y_t &:& \text{Measurement (of altitude) made at time t} \\ \hat{x}_t &:& \text{Estimate of the true state at time t} \end{array}$$
Since the trajectory points are generated at 100Hz, the sensor samples measurements at 100Hz. This doesn't meet the minimum Nyquist frequency criteria. With $\alpha=0.95$, the cut off frequency becomes ~15Hz using the following approximation: $$ \alpha \approx \frac{2\pi f_c}{f_s} $$
